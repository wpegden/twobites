\begin{helper}
Fix a finite branch-label type \(\mathcal T\), a finite transcript family
\(\mathcal P\), a nonnegative common factor \(M\), and formal cell weights
\[
w_{\beta,\tau}
\qquad(\beta\in\mathcal T,\ \tau\in\mathcal P).
\]
Let
\[
\mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)
\]
be the predicate that the formal branch/transcript pair is an actual
nonempty fixed support-pair cell.  Assume every non-realized formal pair has
zero weight:
\[
\neg\mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)\Longrightarrow w_{\beta,\tau}=0.
\]
Assume that on realized pairs there is a residual mass
\(r_{\beta,\tau}\) satisfying the termwise domination
\[
w_{\beta,\tau}\le M r_{\beta,\tau}
\]
and the realized-domain product-mass bound
\[
\sum_{\beta\in\mathcal T}
  \sum_{\tau\in\mathcal P:\mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)}
    M r_{\beta,\tau}
\le M .
\]
Then the full formal branch/transcript sum also satisfies
\[
\sum_{\beta\in\mathcal T}\sum_{\tau\in\mathcal P}
  w_{\beta,\tau}\le M .
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
For a fixed branch label \(\beta\), split the finite transcript sum into
realized and non-realized transcripts.  Every non-realized transcript has
weight \(0\) by hypothesis, so
\[
\sum_{\tau\in\mathcal P}w_{\beta,\tau}
=
\sum_{\tau\in\mathcal P:\mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)}
  w_{\beta,\tau}.
\]
Summing this equality over all \(\beta\in\mathcal T\) gives an equality
between the full formal weight sum and the realized-domain weight sum.

On each realized pair the residual domination hypothesis gives
\[
w_{\beta,\tau}\le M r_{\beta,\tau}.
\]
Finite-sum monotonicity over the realized-domain index set therefore gives
\[
\sum_{\beta\in\mathcal T}
  \sum_{\tau\in\mathcal P:\mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)}
    w_{\beta,\tau}
\le
\sum_{\beta\in\mathcal T}
  \sum_{\tau\in\mathcal P:\mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)}
    M r_{\beta,\tau}.
\]
The right-hand side is at most \(M\) by the realized-domain product-mass
certificate.  Combining the zero-extension equality with this monotone
comparison proves
\[
\sum_{\beta\in\mathcal T}\sum_{\tau\in\mathcal P}w_{\beta,\tau}\le M.
\]
This is exactly the reindexing step needed when the geometry, transcript
conditions, and residual domination have only been established for realized
fixed support-pair cells.
\end{proof}
