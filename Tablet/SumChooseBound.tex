\begin{helper}
Let \(\alpha,\beta\) be types with decidable equality, let \(I\) be a finite
set of elements of \(\alpha\), let \(\pi:\alpha\to\beta\), and let
\(B\in\mathbb R\).  Suppose that for every \(a\in I\),
\[
\left|\{b\in I:\pi(a)=\pi(b)\}\right|\le B .
\]
Then the number of ordered pairs in \(I\times I\) with equal projection is
bounded by
\[
\left|\{(a,b)\in I\times I:\pi(a)=\pi(b)\}\right|
   \le |I|\,B .
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
For each \(a\in I\), put
\[
F_a=\{b\in I:\pi(a)=\pi(b)\}
\]
and let
\[
E_a=\{a\}\times F_a
  =\{(a,b):b\in I,\ \pi(a)=\pi(b)\}.
\]
The sets \(E_a\), as \(a\) ranges over \(I\), are pairwise disjoint: if
\((x,y)\in E_a\cap E_{a'}\), then the first coordinate is both \(a\) and
\(a'\), hence \(a=a'\).  Their union is exactly
\[
E=\{(a,b)\in I\times I:\pi(a)=\pi(b)\},
\]
because an ordered pair in \(E\) belongs to the unique summand indexed by its
first coordinate.

The map \(b\mapsto(a,b)\) is injective from \(F_a\) to \(E_a\), and its image
is all of \(E_a\), so \(|E_a|=|F_a|\).  By finite additivity over the
pairwise-disjoint union,
\[
|E|=\sum_{a\in I}|E_a|
    =\sum_{a\in I}|F_a|.
\]
The hypothesis gives \(|F_a|\le B\) for each \(a\in I\).  Summing these
inequalities yields
\[
|E|\le\sum_{a\in I}B=|I|\,B,
\]
which is the desired bound.
\end{proof}
