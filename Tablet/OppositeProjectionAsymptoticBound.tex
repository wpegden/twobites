\begin{lemma}
Let \(M(n)=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n)\), and let
\(L=\log n\).  For any constant \(c>0\),
\[
M(n)^2\exp(-cL^3)\to0
\]
as \(n\to\infty\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For all sufficiently large \(n\), the reduced vertex count satisfies
\(M(n)\le n\).  Since \(n=\exp(L)\), we have
\[
M(n)^2\exp(-cL^3)\le n^2\exp(-cL^3)=\exp(2L-cL^3).
\]
Because \(c>0\), the cubic term dominates the linear term:
\[
2L-cL^3\to-\infty .
\]
The exponential tends to \(0\) along exponents tending to \(-\infty\), so
\(\exp(2L-cL^3)\to0\).  The squeeze estimate above proves the claimed
convergence.
\end{proof}
