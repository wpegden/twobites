\begin{helper}
Let \(\Omega\) be finite and let \(q:\Omega\to\mathbb R\) be nonnegative with
\(\sum_a q(a)=1\).  Let \(Z:\Omega^r\to\mathbb R\), and suppose that changing
one coordinate of an outcome while keeping all other coordinates fixed changes
\(Z\) by at most \(c\), where \(c>0\).  With
\[
w(\omega)=\prod_{i=0}^{r-1}q(\omega_i),
\qquad
\mu=\sum_{\omega\in\Omega^r}w(\omega)Z(\omega),
\]
one has, for every \(\lambda\ge0\),
\[
\sum_{\omega\in\Omega^r} w(\omega)e^{\lambda(Z(\omega)-\mu)}
\le
\exp\!\left(\frac{r\lambda^2c^2}{8}\right).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
We prove the statement by induction on the number \(r\) of coordinates.  When
\(r=0\), there is only one product outcome.  The expectation is the value of
\(Z\) at that outcome, so \(Z-\mu=0\), the left side is \(1\), and the right
side is also \(1\).

For the induction step, write an outcome as \((x,\eta)\), where \(x\in\Omega\)
is the first coordinate and \(\eta\in\Omega^n\) is the tail.  Let
\[
\mu_x=\sum_{\eta\in\Omega^n}
\left(\prod_{i=0}^{n-1}q(\eta_i)\right)Z(x,\eta).
\]
The product mass splits as \(q(x)\prod_i q(\eta_i)\), and the total
expectation splits as \(\mu=\sum_x q(x)\mu_x\).

For each fixed \(x\), the tail function \(\eta\mapsto Z(x,\eta)\) still has
coordinate oscillation at most \(c\).  The induction hypothesis therefore gives
\[
\sum_{\eta}
\left(\prod_i q(\eta_i)\right)
e^{\lambda(Z(x,\eta)-\mu_x)}
\le
\exp\!\left(\frac{n\lambda^2c^2}{8}\right).
\]
It remains to control the first-coordinate averages \(\mu_x-\mu\).  If
\(x,y\in\Omega\), then
\[
|\mu_x-\mu_y|
\le
\sum_\eta\left(\prod_i q(\eta_i)\right)|Z(x,\eta)-Z(y,\eta)|
\le c,
\]
using the coordinate oscillation hypothesis in the first coordinate and
\noderef{ProductWeightSumOne}.  Hence the values \(\mu_x-\mu\) lie in an
interval of length at most \(c\), and their \(q\)-weighted mean is \(0\).
Applying \noderef{ProductHoeffdingMgFStep} gives
\[
\sum_x q(x)e^{\lambda(\mu_x-\mu)}
\le
\exp\!\left(\frac{\lambda^2c^2}{8}\right).
\]
Multiplying the tail estimate by this first-coordinate estimate yields
\[
\sum_{x,\eta}q(x)\prod_i q(\eta_i)e^{\lambda(Z(x,\eta)-\mu)}
\le
\exp\!\left(\frac{n\lambda^2c^2}{8}\right)
\exp\!\left(\frac{\lambda^2c^2}{8}\right)
=
\exp\!\left(\frac{(n+1)\lambda^2c^2}{8}\right).
\]
This is the induction step.
\end{proof}
