\begin{helper}
Let \(\Omega\) be finite and let \(q:\Omega\to\mathbb R\) be nonnegative with
\(\sum_a q(a)=1\).  Let \(Z:\Omega^r\to\mathbb R\), define
\[
w(\omega)=\prod_{i=0}^{r-1}q(\omega_i),
\qquad
\mu=\sum_{\omega\in\Omega^r}w(\omega)Z(\omega),
\]
and assume that for every \(\lambda>0\),
\[
\sum_\omega w(\omega)e^{\lambda(Z(\omega)-\mu)}
\le
\exp\!\left(\frac{r\lambda^2c^2}{8}\right).
\]
If \(c>0\) and \(t\ge0\), then
\[
\sum_{\omega: \mu+t\le Z(\omega)}w(\omega)
\le
\exp\!\left(-\frac{2t^2}{r c^2}\right),
\]
with the \(r=0\) Lean real-field case interpreted as the trivial right hand
side \(e^0=1\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \noderef{ProductWeightSumOne}, the total mass of \(w\) is \(1\), so the
tail mass is at most \(1\).  This proves the claim when \(t=0\).

Assume \(t>0\).  For any \(\lambda>0\) and any outcome in the tail,
\(t\le Z(\omega)-\mu\), hence
\[
1\le e^{\lambda(Z(\omega)-\mu-t)}
=e^{-\lambda t}e^{\lambda(Z(\omega)-\mu)}.
\]
Multiplying by the nonnegative weight \(w(\omega)\), summing over the tail,
and then enlarging the sum to all outcomes gives
\[
\sum_{\omega:\mu+t\le Z(\omega)}w(\omega)
\le
e^{-\lambda t}
\sum_\omega w(\omega)e^{\lambda(Z(\omega)-\mu)}
\le
\exp\!\left(-\lambda t+\frac{r\lambda^2c^2}{8}\right).
\]
If \(r=0\), taking \(\lambda=1\) gives a bound at most \(e^{-t}\le1\), which
is the displayed right hand side under Lean's real division convention.  If
\(r>0\), the denominator \(r c^2\) is positive, and choosing
\(\lambda=4t/(r c^2)\) minimizes the quadratic exponent.  Substituting this
value gives exactly
\[
-\lambda t+\frac{r\lambda^2c^2}{8}
=-\frac{2t^2}{r c^2}.
\]
\end{proof}
