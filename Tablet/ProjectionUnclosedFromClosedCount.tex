\begin{helper}
Let \(P_I=\noderef{TwoBiteProjectionPairSet}(\omega,I)\), let
\[
C_I=\{e\in P_I:\noderef{TwoBiteProjectionPairClosed}(\omega,I,e)\},
\]
and let \(U_I=\noderef{TwoBiteUnclosedProjectedPairs}(\omega,I)\).  For any
real numbers \(f\) and \(E\), if
\[
|C_I|\le |P_I|-f+E,
\]
then
\[
f-E\le |U_I|.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By definition, \(C_I\) is the filter of \(P_I\) by the predicate
\(\noderef{TwoBiteProjectionPairClosed}(\omega,I,e)\), while \(U_I\) is the
filter of the same finite set \(P_I\) by the negation of that predicate.
Thus \(C_I\) and \(U_I\) are complementary filters inside \(P_I\).  The usual
finite-set filter identity gives
\[
|C_I|+|U_I|=|P_I|.
\]
After viewing these natural cardinalities as real numbers, this is
\[
|U_I|=|P_I|-|C_I|.
\]
The assumed inequality \(|C_I|\le |P_I|-f+E\) is equivalent, by subtracting
it from \(|P_I|\), to
\[
|P_I|-|C_I|\ge f-E.
\]
Substituting \(|U_I|=|P_I|-|C_I|\) gives \(f-E\le |U_I|\), as claimed.
\end{proof}
