\begin{helper}[FiberAndDegreeMixedLiftedIntersectionConditioning]
\label{helper:FiberAndDegreeMixedLiftedIntersectionConditioning}
Conditioning on both base graphs bounds the unconditional probability.
If an event has conditional probability bounded by $C$ for all fixed base graphs $G_R$ and $G_B$, then its unconditional probability is also bounded by $C$.
\end{helper}

\begin{proof}
The unconditional probability is the sum of the conditional probabilities weighted by the marginal probabilities of the conditioning events. Since the conditional probabilities are bounded by $C$ and the marginal probabilities sum to at most $1$, the unconditional probability is bounded by $C$.
\end{proof}
