\begin{helper}
Let \(B\) be a finite index set and let \(\{A_x:x\in B\}\) be finite sets.
Suppose that every two distinct indices satisfy
\[
|A_x\cap A_y|\le C.
\]
Then
\[
\left|\bigcup_{x\in B}A_x\right|
\ge
\sum_{x\in B}|A_x|-\binom{|B|}{2}_{\mathbb R}C.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite set \(B\).  The empty case is immediate.  For the
inductive step write \(B=\{a\}\cup S\), with \(a\notin S\), and put
\(U=\bigcup_{x\in S}A_x\).  The finite-cardinality identity
\[
|A_a\cup U|+|A_a\cap U|=|A_a|+|U|
\]
gives, after casting to real numbers,
\[
|A_a\cup U|=|A_a|+|U|-|A_a\cap U|.
\]
The intersection \(A_a\cap U\) is contained in
\[
\bigcup_{y\in S}(A_a\cap A_y),
\]
so the finite union bound and the pairwise-overlap hypothesis give
\[
|A_a\cap U|\le\sum_{y\in S}|A_a\cap A_y|\le |S|C.
\]
By the induction hypothesis,
\[
|U|\ge\sum_{x\in S}|A_x|-\binom{|S|}{2}_{\mathbb R}C.
\]
Combining these inequalities yields
\[
|A_a\cup U|
\ge |A_a|+\sum_{x\in S}|A_x|
  -\left(\binom{|S|}{2}_{\mathbb R}+|S|\right)C.
\]
Finally
\[
\binom{|S|+1}{2}_{\mathbb R}
=\binom{|S|}{2}_{\mathbb R}+|S|,
\]
which is exactly the asserted bound for \(B=\{a\}\cup S\).
\end{proof}
