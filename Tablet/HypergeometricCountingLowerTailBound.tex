\begin{helper}
Let \(N,n,m\) be natural numbers with \(n\le N\) and \(m\le N\).  Let
\(M\subseteq \operatorname{Fin}(N)\) have cardinality \(m\), and let \(Y\)
count the number of marked elements in a uniformly chosen \(n\)-subset.  Put
\[
q=\frac{m}{N},\qquad \mu=nq.
\]
Here \(q=0\) when \(N=0\), matching the real-division convention.
For every \(0<\delta<1\), the proportion of \(n\)-subsets \(S\) with
\[
|S\cap M|<(1-\delta)\mu
\]
is at most
\[
\exp\!\left(-\bigl(\delta+(1-\delta)\log(1-\delta)\bigr)\mu\right).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let \(L=\log(1-\delta)\).  Since \(0<\delta<1\), we have
\(0<1-\delta<1\), hence \(L<0\) and \(e^L=1-\delta\).  Write
\(a=(1-\delta)\mu\), and let \(E\) be the set of \(n\)-subsets with
\(|S\cap M|<a\).

For every \(S\in E\), the inequality \(L<0\) reverses the comparison and gives
\[
L a < L|S\cap M|.
\]
After exponentiating and summing over \(E\),
\[
|E|e^{La}
\le \sum_{S\in E} e^{L|S\cap M|}
\le \sum_{S\in \binom{\operatorname{Fin}(N)}{n}} e^{L|S\cap M|}.
\]
Dividing by \(\binom Nn\) and by \(e^{La}>0\) gives
\[
\frac{|E|}{\binom Nn}
\le
\left(\binom{N}{n}^{-1}
\sum_{S\in \binom{\operatorname{Fin}(N)}{n}} e^{L|S\cap M|}\right)
e^{-La}.
\]
By \(\noderef{HypergeometricMgfComparison}\), the parenthesized moment
generating function is at most
\[
\exp\bigl(\mu(e^L-1)\bigr).
\]
Therefore
\[
\frac{|E|}{\binom Nn}
\le
\exp\bigl(\mu(e^L-1)-La\bigr).
\]
Substituting \(e^L=1-\delta\), \(L=\log(1-\delta)\), and
\(a=(1-\delta)\mu\), the exponent becomes
\[
-\delta\mu-(1-\delta)\mu\log(1-\delta)
=-\bigl(\delta+(1-\delta)\log(1-\delta)\bigr)\mu,
\]
which is the claimed bound.
\end{proof}
