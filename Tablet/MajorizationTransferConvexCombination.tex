\begin{helper}
Let \(N\) be a natural number, and let \(b, x \in \mathbb R^{\operatorname{Fin}(N)}\). Suppose \(i < j\) are indices such that \(b_i \ge b_j\), \(x_i > b_i\), and \(x_j < b_j\). Let \(\delta > 0\) be a real number such that \(\delta \le x_i - b_i\) and \(\delta \le b_j - x_j\). Let \(y\) be the vector obtained from \(x\) by moving \(\delta\) units from coordinate \(i\) to coordinate \(j\): \(y_i = x_i - \delta\), \(y_j = x_j + \delta\), and \(y_k = x_k\) for \(k \neq i, j\). Then there exists \(\theta \in [0, 1]\) such that for all \(k\), \(y_k = (1 - \theta) x_k + \theta x_{\tau(k)}\), where \(\tau\) is the transposition swapping \(i\) and \(j\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Because \(b_i \ge b_j\), \(x_i > b_i\), and \(x_j < b_j\), we have \(x_i > x_j\). Define
\[
\theta = \frac{\delta}{x_i - x_j}.
\]
The denominator \(x_i - x_j = (x_i - b_i) + (b_i - b_j) + (b_j - x_j)\) is positive since all three terms are nonnegative and the first and third are positive. The bounds \(\delta \le x_i - b_i\) and \(\delta \le b_j - x_j\) imply that \(0 < \delta \le x_i - x_j\), so \(0 \le \theta \le 1\).

For \(k = i\), we have
\[
(1 - \theta) x_i + \theta x_j = x_i - \theta(x_i - x_j) = x_i - \delta = y_i.
\]
For \(k = j\), we have
\[
(1 - \theta) x_j + \theta x_i = x_j + \theta(x_i - x_j) = x_j + \delta = y_j.
\]
For \(k \neq i, j\), we have \(\tau(k) = k\), so
\[
(1 - \theta) x_k + \theta x_{\tau(k)} = x_k = y_k.
\]
Thus, \(y\) is a convex combination of \(x\) and its permutation by \(\tau\).
\end{proof}
