\begin{helper}
Let \(\alpha\) be a type with decidable equality, let
\(r:\alpha\to\mathbb N\) be a rank function, let \(I\) be a finite set, and
let \(P\) be a decidable predicate on ordered pairs in \(\alpha\times\alpha\).
Assume \(P\) is symmetric, in the sense that
\[
P(a,b)\quad\Longrightarrow\quad P(b,a)
\]
for all \(a,b\).  Then
\[
2\left|
  \{(a,b)\in\noderef{TwoBitePairsInSet}(r,I):P(a,b)\}
\right|
\le
\left|\{(a,b)\in I\times I:P(a,b)\}\right|.
\]
Equivalently, the rank-oriented representatives in
\(\noderef{TwoBitePairsInSet}(r,I)\) contribute at most one half of the
ordered-product count once the predicate is symmetric.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let
\[
A=\{(a,b)\in\noderef{TwoBitePairsInSet}(r,I):P(a,b)\}.
\]
By the definition of \(\noderef{TwoBitePairsInSet}\), every \((a,b)\in A\)
satisfies \(a,b\in I\), \(r(a)<r(b)\), and \(P(a,b)\).  Let
\[
\tau(A)=\{(b,a):(a,b)\in A\}
\]
be the swapped copy of \(A\).

First, \(A\) and \(\tau(A)\) are disjoint.  Indeed, if
\((x,y)\in A\cap\tau(A)\), then \((x,y)=(b,a)\) for some
\((a,b)\in A\).  Membership of \((x,y)\) in \(A\) gives \(r(x)<r(y)\), while
membership of \((a,b)\) in \(A\) gives \(r(a)<r(b)\).  Since
\((x,y)=(b,a)\), the latter inequality is \(r(y)<r(x)\), a contradiction.

Second, \(A\cup\tau(A)\) is contained in
\[
\{(a,b)\in I\times I:P(a,b)\}.
\]
This is immediate for elements of \(A\).  If \((x,y)\in\tau(A)\), then
\((x,y)=(b,a)\) for some \((a,b)\in A\).  Since \(a,b\in I\), also
\((b,a)\in I\times I\), and the symmetry hypothesis turns \(P(a,b)\) into
\(P(b,a)\).

Finally, the swap map \((a,b)\mapsto(b,a)\) is injective, so
\(|\tau(A)|=|A|\).  Therefore disjoint additivity gives
\[
|A\cup\tau(A)|=|A|+|\tau(A)|=2|A|.
\]
Because \(A\cup\tau(A)\) is a subset of the ordered-product predicate set,
\[
2|A|=|A\cup\tau(A)|
\le \left|\{(a,b)\in I\times I:P(a,b)\}\right|,
\]
as required.
\end{proof}
