\begin{helper}
Let \(H\) be a finite index set and let \(A_x\subseteq I\) be finite sets
indexed by \(x\in H\), with \(|I|=k\).  Suppose that, for every \(x\in H\),
\[
t<|A_x|\le k,
\]
and that every two distinct \(x,y\in H\) satisfy
\[
|A_x\cap A_y|\le C.
\]
Assume \(t>0\), \(C\ge0\), and
\[
\binom{2k/t+1}{2}_{\mathbb R}C\le\frac12 k.
\]
Then
\[
\sum_{x\in H}|A_x|\le2k,\qquad |H|\le\frac{2k}{t}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Put \(a=2k/t\).  Since \(k\ge0\) and \(t>0\), we have \(a\ge0\).  We first
prove \(|H|\le a\).  Suppose instead that \(|H|>a\).  Let
\[
r=\max\{1,\lceil a\rceil\}.
\]
Then \(1\le r\), \(a\le r\le a+1\), and \(r\le |H|\).  Choose a subset
\(B\subseteq H\) with \(|B|=r\).  Since each \(A_x\) with \(x\in B\) has
cardinality strictly greater than \(t\), summing the strict inequalities gives
\[
\sum_{x\in B}|A_x|>rt\ge at=2k.
\]

By \(\noderef{HugeFamilyFiniteUnionLowerBound}\),
\[
\left|\bigcup_{x\in B}A_x\right|
\ge
\sum_{x\in B}|A_x|-\binom r2_{\mathbb R}C.
\]
The function \(u\mapsto \binom u2_{\mathbb R}=u(u-1)/2\) is increasing on
\([1,\infty)\): for \(1\le u\le v\),
\[
\binom v2_{\mathbb R}-\binom u2_{\mathbb R}
=\frac{(v-u)(u+v-1)}2\ge0.
\]
Because \(1\le r\le a+1\) and \(C\ge0\),
\[
\binom r2_{\mathbb R}C
\le \binom{a+1}{2}_{\mathbb R}C
\le \frac12 k.
\]
Thus
\[
\left|\bigcup_{x\in B}A_x\right|>\frac32 k.
\]
But every \(A_x\) is contained in \(I\), so the union is contained in \(I\)
and has cardinality at most \(k\).  This contradicts \(k\ge0\).  Therefore
\(|H|\le a=2k/t\).

Now apply \(\noderef{HugeFamilyFiniteUnionLowerBound}\) to \(B=H\).  Since
\(|H|\le a\), the same monotonicity argument gives
\[
\binom{|H|}{2}_{\mathbb R}C\le \frac12 k.
\]
The union over \(H\) is contained in \(I\), so its cardinality is at most
\(k\).  Rearranging the lower bound
\[
\left|\bigcup_{x\in H}A_x\right|
\ge
\sum_{x\in H}|A_x|-\binom{|H|}{2}_{\mathbb R}C
\]
therefore yields
\[
\sum_{x\in H}|A_x|
\le k+\binom{|H|}{2}_{\mathbb R}C
\le \frac32 k\le2k.
\]
\end{proof}
