\begin{helper}
For a sequence of two-bite laws, suppose three event sequences \(E_n,F_n,G_n\)
each hold with high probability and, eventually, all sample weights are
nonnegative.  Then the triple intersection \(E_n\cap F_n\cap G_n\) also holds
with high probability.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let \(a_n,b_n,c_n\) be the three event probabilities.  By hypothesis they all
converge to \(1\), so \(a_n+b_n+c_n-2\to1\).  For all sufficiently large \(n\),
the sample weights are nonnegative.  Applying
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbabilityTripleIntersectionLowerBound}\) and
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbabilityTotalMassBound}\) gives
\[
a_n+b_n+c_n-2\le \Pr(E_n\cap F_n\cap G_n).
\]
The same nonnegativity gives monotonicity of the finite weighted sum, so the
intersection probability is at most the total mass of the whole sample space,
which is at most \(1\) by
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbabilityTotalMassBound}\).  The squeeze theorem
therefore gives convergence of the intersection probability to \(1\), which is
exactly the asserted high-probability statement.
\end{proof}
