\begin{helper}
\label{helper:OppositeProjectionRowInjectionLawBridge}
Let \(n,m\in\mathbb N\), \(p\in\mathbb R\), let \(G_R\) be a simple graph on
\(\operatorname{Fin}m\), let \(\rho:\operatorname{Fin}n\to\operatorname{Fin}m\),
let \(S\subseteq\operatorname{Fin}m\), and let
\(T\subseteq\operatorname{Fin}n\).  For a two-bite sample
\(\omega=(G'_R,G_B,\phi)\), define
\[
C=\{\omega:G'_R=G_R\text{ and }(\phi(v))_1=\rho(v)\text{ for every }v\},
\]
and
\[
E=\{\omega:(\phi(v))_2\in S\text{ for every }v\in T\}.
\]
Define
\[
X_{\rm all}
=\{\phi:\operatorname{Fin}n\hookrightarrow
\operatorname{Fin}m\times\operatorname{Fin}m:
(\phi(v))_1=\rho(v)\text{ for every }v\},
\]
and
\[
X_{\rm good}
=\{\phi\in X_{\rm all}:(\phi(v))_2\in S\text{ for every }v\in T\}.
\]
Then
\[
\operatorname{TwoBiteEventProbability}_{n,m,p}(C)
=\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_R)\,
  \operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)\,|X_{\rm all}|,
\]
and
\[
\operatorname{TwoBiteEventProbability}_{n,m,p}(E\cap C)
=\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_R)\,
  \operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)\,|X_{\rm good}|.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
A two-bite sample has the form \(\omega=(G'_R,G_B,\phi)\), and by
\(\noderef{TwoBiteSampleWeight}\) its weight is
\[
\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G'_R)\,
\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_B)\,
\operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m).
\]
Expand \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\) for the conditioning event
\(C\).  The event \(C\) forces \(G'_R=G_R\) and forces
\(\phi\in X_{\rm all}\), while it imposes no condition on the independent
blue graph \(G_B\).  Therefore
\[
\operatorname{TwoBiteEventProbability}_{n,m,p}(C)
=\sum_{G_B}\sum_{\phi\in X_{\rm all}}
  \operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_R)\,
  \operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_B)\,
  \operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m).
\]
The factors \(\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_R)\) and
\(\operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)\) do not depend on \(G_B\) or
\(\phi\), and the inner sum has one identical term for each element of
\(X_{\rm all}\).  Hence the last display equals
\[
\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_R)\,
\operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)\,|X_{\rm all}|
\sum_{G_B}\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_B).
\]
By \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeightSumOne}\), the sum over all blue graph weights is
\(1\).  This proves
\[
\operatorname{TwoBiteEventProbability}_{n,m,p}(C)
=\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_R)\,
  \operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)\,|X_{\rm all}|.
\]

The proof for \(E\cap C\) is the same with the injection sum restricted to
\(X_{\rm good}\).  Indeed, \(E\cap C\) forces \(G'_R=G_R\), forces the first
coordinate condition \((\phi(v))_1=\rho(v)\) for every \(v\), and also forces
\((\phi(v))_2\in S\) for every \(v\in T\).  These are exactly the conditions
defining \(X_{\rm good}\), and again there is no restriction on \(G_B\).  Thus
\[
\operatorname{TwoBiteEventProbability}_{n,m,p}(E\cap C)
=\sum_{G_B}\sum_{\phi\in X_{\rm good}}
  \operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_R)\,
  \operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_B)\,
  \operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m).
\]
Factoring the constant red-graph and injection weights gives
\[
\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_R)\,
\operatorname{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)\,|X_{\rm good}|
\sum_{G_B}\operatorname{GnpGraphWeight}_p(G_B),
\]
and \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeightSumOne}\) again turns the blue-graph sum into
\(1\).  This proves the second displayed identity.
\end{proof}
