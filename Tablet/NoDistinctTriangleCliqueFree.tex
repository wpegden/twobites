\begin{helper}
Let \(G\) be a simple graph on a type \(\alpha\).  If there are no vertices
\(u,v,w\in\alpha\) such that
\[
u\ne v,\qquad u\ne w,\qquad v\ne w
\]
and
\[
G.\mathrm{Adj}(u,v),\qquad
G.\mathrm{Adj}(u,w),\qquad
G.\mathrm{Adj}(v,w),
\]
then \(G.\mathrm{CliqueFree}(3)\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By the Mathlib definition of \(G.\mathrm{CliqueFree}(3)\), it is enough to
show that there is no copy of the complete graph on \(\mathrm{Fin}(3)\) inside
\(G\).  Suppose, for contradiction, that such a copy \(f\) exists.

Let
\[
u=f(0),\qquad v=f(1),\qquad w=f(2),
\]
where \(0,1,2\) are the three distinct elements of \(\mathrm{Fin}(3)\).  Since
a graph copy is injective on vertices, \(u\ne v\), \(u\ne w\), and \(v\ne w\).
Since the source graph is complete, the three source pairs \((0,1)\),
\((0,2)\), and \((1,2)\) are adjacent.  A graph copy maps adjacent source
vertices to adjacent target vertices, so
\[
G.\mathrm{Adj}(u,v),\qquad
G.\mathrm{Adj}(u,w),\qquad
G.\mathrm{Adj}(v,w).
\]
These vertices form exactly the forbidden distinct adjacent triple, contrary
to the hypothesis.  Therefore no such copy of the complete graph on
\(\mathrm{Fin}(3)\) exists, and hence \(G.\mathrm{CliqueFree}(3)\).
\end{proof}
