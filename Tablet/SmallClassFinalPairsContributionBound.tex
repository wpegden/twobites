\begin{helper}[Small-Class Final-Pairs Contribution Bound]
Let \(\omega\) be a two-bite sample and let \(x\) be a base vertex in the
small class for \(I\). If
\[
c_{\mathrm{prod}}=\frac12 n^{4\varepsilon},
\]
then any filtered subset of
\(\noderef{TwoBiteFinalPairs}(\noderef{TwoBiteX}(\omega,I,x))\) has cardinality
at most \(c_{\mathrm{prod}}\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Filtering can only decrease cardinality. By \(\noderef{FinalPairsCardLeHalfSq}\),
\[
|\noderef{TwoBiteFinalPairs}(\noderef{TwoBiteX}(\omega,I,x))|
\le \frac12 |\noderef{TwoBiteX}(\omega,I,x)|^2.
\]
Since \(x\) is in the small class, \(|\noderef{TwoBiteX}(\omega,I,x)|\le
\noderef{TwoBiteSmallCutoff}(\varepsilon,n)=n^{2\varepsilon}\). Squaring this
nonnegative inequality and using \((n^{2\varepsilon})^2=n^{4\varepsilon}\)
gives the desired bound.
\end{proof}
