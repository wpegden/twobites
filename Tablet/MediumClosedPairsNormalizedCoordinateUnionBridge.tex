\begin{lemma}
Fix finite red centers \(B_R\), blue centers \(B_B\), red projections \(P_R\),
and blue projections \(P_B\).  Normalize every nonloop ordered red candidate
\((r,s)\in B_R\times P_R\) to the unordered coordinate
\((\min(r,s),\max(r,s))\), tagged red, and normalize every nonloop ordered
blue candidate \((b,c)\in B_B\times P_B\) in the same way, tagged blue.  Let
\(C\) be the union of these normalized coordinate sets.  Suppose \(0\le p\le1\)
and suppose that every \(t\)-element \(A\subseteq C\) satisfies
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}
\left(\{\omega:\text{every coordinate in }A\text{ is present in }\omega\}\right)
\le p^t .
\]
Then the probability that at least \(t\) coordinates of \(C\) are present is
at most
\[
\binom{|B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|}{t}p^t .
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Let \(R_0\subseteq B_R\times P_R\) and \(B_0\subseteq B_B\times P_B\) be the
nonloop ordered candidate pairs.  The normalized red coordinate set is the
image of \(R_0\), so its cardinality is at most \(|R_0|\).  Since
\(R_0\subseteq B_R\times P_R\), we have \(|R_0|\le |B_R||P_R|\).  The same
argument gives the blue bound \(|B_0|\le |B_B||P_B|\).

The union \(C\) has cardinality at most the sum of the red and blue normalized
coordinate-set cardinalities, hence
\[
|C|\le |B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|.
\]
Applying \(\noderef{MediumClosedPairsCoordinateUnionBridge}\) with this
cardinality bound and with the assumed fixed-subset product-law bound gives
exactly
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}
\left(\{\omega:\text{at least }t\text{ coordinates of }C
\text{ are present}\}\right)
\le
\binom{|B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|}{t}p^t .
\]
\end{proof}
