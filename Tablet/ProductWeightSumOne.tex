\begin{helper}
Let \(\Omega\) be finite and let \(q:\Omega\to\mathbb R\) be nonnegative with
\(\sum_{a\in\Omega}q(a)=1\).  For every \(r\ge 0\), the product weight
\[
w(\omega)=\prod_{i=0}^{r-1}q(\omega_i)
\]
on \(\Omega^r\) has total mass
\[
\sum_{\omega\in\Omega^r} w(\omega)=1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The sum over product outcomes factors coordinate by coordinate.  More
precisely,
\[
\sum_{\omega\in\Omega^r}\prod_{i=0}^{r-1}q(\omega_i)
=\prod_{i=0}^{r-1}\sum_{a\in\Omega}q(a).
\]
Each coordinate factor is \(1\) by the hypothesis on \(q\).  The product of
\(r\) copies of \(1\), including the empty product when \(r=0\), is therefore
\(1\).
\end{proof}
