\begin{lemma}
Assume \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchy}(\varepsilon,\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2,n_0)\).
Let
\[
K(n)=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalIndependenceScale}(1+\varepsilon,n).
\]
Then
\[
\exp\!\left(
K(n)\log n+
2K(n)n^{1/4-3\varepsilon}\log n
-n^{3/4-2\varepsilon}
\right)
\longrightarrow 0 .
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The hierarchy gives \(0<\varepsilon<1/16\).  We prove convergence by the
order characterization of convergence to \(0\).  The exponential envelope is
always positive, so it is eventually above every negative number.

Let \(b>0\).  Put \(\delta=b/2\).  Apply
\(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyBaseThreshold}\) to obtain the eventual rounded
upper bound
\[
K(n)<2(1+\varepsilon)\sqrt{n\log n}.
\]
Apply \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyT28Threshold}\) with tolerance \(\delta\).
After increasing the threshold and also requiring \(n>n_0\),
\(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyLogPositivity}\) gives \(\log n>0\), and the two
T28 threshold inequalities plus
\[
\exp\!\left(-\frac12 n^{3/4-2\varepsilon}\right)\le\delta
\]
hold.  Rewriting \(\sqrt{n\log n}=\sqrt n\,\sqrt{\log n}\), all hypotheses of
\(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyT28Algebra}\) are satisfied with
\(k=K(n)\).  That lemma gives the envelope at \(n\) at most \(\delta\), hence
strictly below \(b\).  Since this works for every \(b>0\), the envelope tends
to \(0\).
\end{proof}
