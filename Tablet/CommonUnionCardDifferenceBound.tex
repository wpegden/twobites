\begin{helper}[Common Union Cardinal Difference Bound]
Let \(U,A,B\) be finite sets. Then
\[
\bigl||U\cup A|-|U\cup B|\bigr|\le \max\{|A|,|B|\}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Write \(U\cup A\) as the disjoint union \(U\sqcup(A\setminus U)\), and similarly
\(U\cup B=U\sqcup(B\setminus U)\). Hence the common \(|U|\) terms cancel:
\[
\bigl||U\cup A|-|U\cup B|\bigr|
=\bigl||A\setminus U|-|B\setminus U|\bigr|.
\]
For nonnegative integers \(a,b\), \(|a-b|\le\max\{a,b\}\). Applying this to
\(|A\setminus U|\) and \(|B\setminus U|\), and then using
\(|A\setminus U|\le |A|\) and \(|B\setminus U|\le |B|\), gives the claim.
\end{proof}
