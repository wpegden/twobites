\begin{helper}
For every \(\varepsilon_1>0\), there is \(n_0\) such that for every
\(n>n_0\), if \(L=\log n\), then
\[
\frac{2\log L}{L^2}\le\frac{\varepsilon_1}{10}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(L=\log n\to\infty\), choose \(n_0\) so that for every \(n>n_0\),
\[
L\ge 1,\qquad L\ge \frac{20}{\varepsilon_1}.
\]
For \(L\ge1\), the elementary bound \(\log L\le L\) gives
\[
\frac{2\log L}{L^2}\le\frac{2L}{L^2}=\frac2L.
\]
The second threshold condition gives \(20\le\varepsilon_1 L\), equivalently
\[
\frac2L\le\frac{\varepsilon_1}{10}.
\]
Combining the two displayed inequalities proves the claim.
\end{proof}
