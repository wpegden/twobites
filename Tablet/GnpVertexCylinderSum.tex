\begin{helper}[GnpVertexCylinderSum]
Let \(m\) be a natural number, let \(p\in\mathbb R\), let
\(S\subseteq \operatorname{Fin}(m)\) be finite, and let
\(r\in\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) with \(r\notin S\).  Under the
\(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}\) product graph weight, the cylinder event that
all edges from \(r\) to vertices in \(S\) are present has mass
\[
\sum_G
\begin{cases}
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G),
&\text{if }\forall t\in S,\;G.Adj(r,t),\\
0,&\text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
=p^{|S|}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Use the edge-coordinate representation from \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}\):
each unordered edge is represented by the ordered pair \((a,b)\) with
\(a<b\), and a graph is equivalently a Boolean assignment to these
coordinates.  The event in the statement requires exactly the coordinates
\(\{r,t\}\) for \(t\in S\) to be present.  Since \(r\notin S\), these are
\(|S|\) distinct non-loop coordinates.

For each required coordinate, summing over the Boolean value with the
constraint that the edge is present contributes \(p\).  For every other edge
coordinate, the two choices contribute \(p+(1-p)=1\); this is the finite
product-sum collapse encoded by \(\noderef{ProductWeightSumOne}\).  Thus the
full graph sum factors as \(p^{|S|}\) times a product of \(1\)'s over all
unconstrained coordinates, which is \(p^{|S|}\).
\end{proof}
