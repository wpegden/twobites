\begin{helper}
Let \(B\) and \(R\) be the fixed blue and red present supports of sizes
\(u_B\) and \(u_R\).  Let \(\tau=(P,A,Z)\) be a transcript with
\[
B\subseteq P,\qquad R\subseteq P,\qquad B\cap R=\emptyset,
\]
\[
Z\subseteq A,\qquad P\cap A=\emptyset,
\]
and
\[
L:=\max(0,a-u_R-u_B)\le |Z|.
\]
Assume \(0\le p\le1/2\).  Suppose the residual leaf mass \(\nu\) dominates
the factors left after the mandatory present support \(B\cup R\) and the
selected absences \(Z\) have been removed:
\[
p^{|P|-u_R-u_B}(1-p)^{|A|-|Z|}\le \nu .
\]
Then the full cylinder factor satisfies the termwise domination
\[
p^{|P|}(1-p)^{|A|}
\le
p^{u_R}p^{u_B}
(1-p)^L\,\nu .
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Write \(q=1-p\).  From \(0\le p\le1/2\) we have \(0\le p\le1\) and
\(0\le q\le1\).  Since \(B\) and \(R\) are disjoint subsets of \(P\), with
cardinalities \(u_B\) and \(u_R\), the present part of the cylinder factors
as
\[
p^{|P|}
=
p^{u_B}p^{u_R}p^{|P|-u_B-u_R}.
\]
Since \(Z\subseteq A\), the absent part factors as
\[
q^{|A|}=q^{|Z|}q^{|A|-|Z|}.
\]
Multiplying these two identities gives
\[
p^{|P|}q^{|A|}
=
p^{u_R}p^{u_B}q^{|Z|}
  \bigl(p^{|P|-u_R-u_B}q^{|A|-|Z|}\bigr).
\]
By the residual domination hypothesis, the parenthesized residual factor is
at most \(\nu\).  All factors are nonnegative, so multiplication by
\(p^{u_R}p^{u_B}q^{|Z|}\) preserves the inequality.

Finally, \(L\le |Z|\) and \(0\le q\le1\), so \(q^{|Z|}\le q^L\).  Replacing
the selected-absence factor by the larger common factor \(q^L\) gives
\[
p^{|P|}q^{|A|}
\le
p^{u_R}p^{u_B}q^L\nu .
\]
Substituting back \(q=1-p\) proves the claimed termwise cylinder
domination.  The assumptions \(0\le p\le1/2\) and the residual domination
hypothesis are part of this node's statement; they are not consequences of
the purely combinatorial branch/transcript construction.
\end{proof}
