\begin{helper}
For every fixed real number \(c>0\), eventually in \(k\),
\[
\frac{\log\bigl(c\,\noderef{RamseyScale}(k)\bigr)}{\log k}
=2+\frac{\log c}{\log k}-\frac{\log\log k}{\log k}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Fix \(c>0\).  For all sufficiently large \(k\), we have \(k>1\), so
\(k>0\) and \(\log k>0\).  Hence \(k\ne0\), \(\log k\ne0\), and
\[
\noderef{RamseyScale}(k)=\frac{k^2}{\log k}
\]
is nonzero.  The logarithm laws for nonzero real arguments give
\[
\begin{aligned}
\log\bigl(c\,\noderef{RamseyScale}(k)\bigr)
&=\log c+\log\left(\frac{k^2}{\log k}\right)\\
&=\log c+\log(k^2)-\log(\log k)\\
&=\log c+2\log k-\log\log k.
\end{aligned}
\]
Dividing by \(\log k\ne0\) and simplifying yields
\[
\frac{\log\bigl(c\,\noderef{RamseyScale}(k)\bigr)}{\log k}
=2+\frac{\log c}{\log k}-\frac{\log\log k}{\log k}.
\]
\end{proof}
