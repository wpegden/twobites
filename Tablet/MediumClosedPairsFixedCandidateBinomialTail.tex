\begin{lemma}
Fix a finite set of \(N\) candidate edges and Bernoulli parameter \(p\ge0\).
Let \(A>0\), put \(t=\lceil A\rceil\), and suppose the mean
\(\mu=pN\) satisfies
\[
\mu\le \frac{A}{4e}.
\]
If a fixed-candidate bad event has probability, or product weight, at most the
\(t\)-subset union-bound quantity
\[
\binom Nt p^t,
\]
then that bad event has probability, or product weight, at most
\[
\exp(-A/4).
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The \(t\)-subset union bound gives the assumed upper bound
\(\binom Nt p^t\).  The standard binomial coefficient estimate gives
\[
\binom Nt p^t\le \frac{N^t p^t}{t!}
  =\frac{\mu^t}{t!}.
\]
The exponential-series inequality \(t^t/t!\le e^t\) gives
\[
\frac{\mu^t}{t!}\le \left(\frac{e\mu}{t}\right)^t.
\]
Since \(t=\lceil A\rceil\), we have \(A\le t\).  Together with
\(\mu\le A/(4e)\), this implies
\[
\frac{e\mu}{t}\le\frac14.
\]
Therefore
\[
\left(\frac{e\mu}{t}\right)^t\le 4^{-t}.
\]
Finally,
\[
4^{-t}=\exp(t\log(1/4))
\le \exp(A\log(1/4))
\le \exp(-A/4),
\]
because \(\log(1/4)\le -1/4\) and \(A\le t\).  Combining the displayed
inequalities proves the claim.
\end{proof}
