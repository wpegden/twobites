\begin{helper}
Let \(\mathcal C\) and \(\Lambda\) be finite types.  Think of
\(\mathcal C\) as a finite set of cells and \(\Lambda\) as a finite set of
residual leaves, with decidable equality on \(\Lambda\).  Let
\[
w:\mathcal C\to\mathbb R,\qquad
\iota:\mathcal C\to\Lambda,\qquad
\mu:\Lambda\to\mathbb R
\]
be respectively a cell-weight function, a cell-to-leaf map, and a leaf-mass
function.  Let \(M\in\mathbb R\).  Assume
\[
0\le M,
\]
\[
0\le \mu(\lambda)\quad\text{for every }\lambda\in\Lambda,
\]
\[
\sum_{\lambda\in\Lambda}\mu(\lambda)\le1,
\]
that \(\iota\) is injective, and that every cell weight is termwise dominated
by the mass of its assigned leaf:
\[
w(c)\le M\,\mu(\iota(c))
\quad\text{for every }c\in\mathcal C.
\]
Then there is a residual-mass function \(r:\mathcal C\to\mathbb R\) such
that
\[
\sum_{c\in\mathcal C} M r(c)\le M
\]
and
\[
w(c)\le M r(c)
\quad\text{for every }c\in\mathcal C.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Define
\[
r(c)=\mu(\iota(c))
\]
for every cell \(c\in\mathcal C\).  With this definition, the required
termwise domination is exactly the assumed inequality
\[
w(c)\le M\,\mu(\iota(c))=M r(c).
\]

It remains to prove the total residual-mass bound.  Since \(\iota\) is
injective, summing \(M\mu(\iota(c))\) over \(c\in\mathcal C\) is the same as
summing \(M\mu(\lambda)\) over the image \(\iota(\mathcal C)\), with no
leaf counted twice:
\[
\sum_{c\in\mathcal C} M r(c)
=
\sum_{\lambda\in\iota(\mathcal C)} M\mu(\lambda).
\]
The image \(\iota(\mathcal C)\) is a subset of the full finite leaf type
\(\Lambda\).  For every leaf \(\lambda\), the hypotheses \(0\le M\) and
\(0\le\mu(\lambda)\) give
\[
0\le M\mu(\lambda).
\]
Therefore enlarging the sum from \(\iota(\mathcal C)\) to all of
\(\Lambda\) can only increase it:
\[
\sum_{\lambda\in\iota(\mathcal C)} M\mu(\lambda)
\le
\sum_{\lambda\in\Lambda} M\mu(\lambda).
\]
Factoring out the constant \(M\) gives
\[
\sum_{\lambda\in\Lambda} M\mu(\lambda)
=
M\sum_{\lambda\in\Lambda}\mu(\lambda).
\]
Finally, using \(\sum_{\lambda\in\Lambda}\mu(\lambda)\le1\) and
\(0\le M\),
\[
M\sum_{\lambda\in\Lambda}\mu(\lambda)\le M\cdot1=M.
\]
Combining these displayed inequalities proves
\[
\sum_{c\in\mathcal C} M r(c)\le M.
\]
Together with the termwise domination already proved, this is the desired
residual-mass certificate.
\end{proof}
