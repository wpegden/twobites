\begin{helper}
For every \(n,m\in\mathbb N\), every \(p\in\mathbb R\), the total
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\) mass of the whole two-bite sample space is
at most \(1\):
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m,p,\mathrm{True})\le 1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the two-bite sample weight.  The total mass factors as the product of
the red graph total mass, the blue graph total mass, and the total mass of the
uniform injection coordinate.  By \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeightSumOne}\), each graph
coordinate has total mass \(1\).  If there are no injections, the injection
weight is \(0\), so its total mass is \(0\).  Otherwise the injection weight is
the reciprocal of the number of injections, and summing this constant over all
injections gives \(1\).  Hence the full product is either \(0\) or \(1\), and in
particular is at most \(1\).
\end{proof}
