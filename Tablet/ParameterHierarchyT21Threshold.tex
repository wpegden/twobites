\begin{helper}
For every \(\eta>0\) with \(\eta<1/16\), there is \(n_0\) such that for
every \(n>n_0\), if \(L=\log n\), then
\[
\frac{\log L}{n^{1/4-\eta}\sqrt L}<1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The exponent \(1/4-\eta\) is positive because \(\eta<1/16<1/4\).
Since \(\log n=o(n^{1/4-\eta})\), enlarge \(n_0\) so that for every
\(n>n_0\),
\[
\log n\le \frac12 n^{1/4-\eta}
\quad\text{and}\quad
L=\log n\ge1.
\]
Then \(\sqrt L\ge1\), and \(\log L\le L=\log n\).  Therefore
\[
\log L\le L\le \frac12 n^{1/4-\eta}
< n^{1/4-\eta}\le n^{1/4-\eta}\sqrt L.
\]
Dividing by the positive denominator \(n^{1/4-\eta}\sqrt L\) gives the
claimed strict inequality.
\end{proof}
