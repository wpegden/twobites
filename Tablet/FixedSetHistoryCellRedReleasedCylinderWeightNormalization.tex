\begin{helper}
Fix the same data as in the red released-cylinder normalization node:
the history cell \(H\), terminal coordinate set \(U\), fixed blue support
\(B\), red label \(J\), branch labels \(\beta\), and transcript labels
\(\tau=(P_\tau,A_\tau,Z_\tau)\).  Assume the complete terminal product law,
the fixed support hypotheses, branch functionality from complete blue traces,
the realized-cell geometry, the open-candidate support exhaustion, the
selected-sibling pruning clause, the span and disjointness hypotheses for the
enlarged released cylinders, and the forward, reverse, and functional
compatibility clauses.

Then the total released-cylinder product weight over all realized
branch/transcript pairs is bounded by the fixed red and blue present support
weight:
\[
\sum_{\substack{\beta,\tau\in\mathcal T\\ \mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)}}
  p^{|P_\tau|}(1-p)^{|A_\tau|-|Z_\tau|}
\le p^{u_R}p^{u_B}.
\]
The selected absences \(Z_\tau\) are released and therefore do not contribute
to the absent factor in this released-cylinder weight.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let \(q=1-p\).  For each realized \((\beta,\tau)\), let
\(C_{\beta,\tau}\) be the enlarged released cylinder of complete terminal
assignments \(A\subseteq U\): the coordinates in \(P_\tau\) are present, the
coordinates in \(A_\tau\setminus Z_\tau\) are absent, and the blue terminal
truth table outside \(Z_\tau\) agrees with
\(\operatorname{blueTrace}(\beta)\).  The realized-cell geometry says that
every coordinate of \(Z_\tau\) is red, so this release does not remove any
blue absent constraint.

The span hypothesis identifies \(C_{\beta,\tau}\) with the compatible
assignments for the realized cell together with the selected present sibling
pieces.  The selected-sibling pruning hypothesis says that a selected present
sibling assignment is not compatible with any realized cell.  The disjointness
hypothesis for the span, together with the branch/scan functionality
\(\noderef{FixedSetHistoryCellRedFunctionalScanDisjointness}\), gives
pairwise disjointness of the released cylinders for distinct realized
\((\beta,\tau)\).

By the terminal product partition
\(\noderef{FixedSetHistoryCellTerminalProductPartition}\), the product weight
of \(C_{\beta,\tau}\) is
\[
\mu(C_{\beta,\tau})
=p^{|P_\tau|}q^{|A_\tau|-|Z_\tau|}.
\]
The fixed support hypotheses give \(B\cup R_{\beta,J}\subseteq P_\tau\),
\(|B|=u_B\), \(|R_{\beta,J}|=u_R\), and blue/red color separation.  The red
support \(R_{\beta,J}\) is determined once the complete blue trace is fixed,
by \(\noderef{FixedSetHistoryCellRedSupportDeterminedByBlueTrace}\).  Hence,
after summing over mutually exclusive complete blue traces, the union
\[
E=\bigcup_{\mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)} C_{\beta,\tau}
\]
has product weight at most \(p^{u_R}p^{u_B}\).
Since the cylinders are pairwise disjoint and have product weights
\[
\mu(C_{\beta,\tau})
=p^{|P_\tau|}q^{|A_\tau|-|Z_\tau|},
\]
finite additivity gives
\[
\sum_{\mathsf{Realized}(\beta,\tau)}
  p^{|P_\tau|}q^{|A_\tau|-|Z_\tau|}
=\mu(E)\le p^{u_R}p^{u_B}.
\]
This is exactly the asserted finite union weight bound.
\end{proof}
