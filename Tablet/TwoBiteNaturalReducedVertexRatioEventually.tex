\begin{helper}[Natural reduced vertex ratio]
For \(m(n)=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n)\), eventually
\[
n\le 2(\log n)^2 m(n).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Unfolding \(\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}\),
\(\noderef{TwoBiteReducedVertexCount}\), and
\(\noderef{TwoBiteStretch}\), we have
\[
m(n)=\left\lfloor \frac{n}{(\log n)^2}\right\rfloor .
\]
For all sufficiently large \(n\), \(\log n\ge 1\) and
\(\log n\le \frac12 n^{1/4}\).  Hence \(2(\log n)^2\le n/(\log n)^2\).
For such \(n\), the elementary floor estimate gives
\[
\frac{n}{2(\log n)^2}\le
\left\lfloor \frac{n}{(\log n)^2}\right\rfloor = m(n).
\]
Multiplying by the positive number \(2(\log n)^2\) gives the claimed
eventual inequality.
\end{proof}
