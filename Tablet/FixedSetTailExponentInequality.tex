\begin{helper}
For any positive reals \(L\) and positive integer \(n\), if \(r=2M\), \(c=\frac{1}{2}n^{4\varepsilon}\), and \(t=\frac{C}{\sqrt{L}}\), then
\[
-\frac{2t^2}{rc^2} \le -\frac{C^2}{LMn^{8\varepsilon}}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Substituting the values of \(r, c, t\) gives
\[
-\frac{2(C/\sqrt{L})^2}{(2M)(\frac{1}{2}n^{4\varepsilon})^2}
= -\frac{2C^2/L}{2M \cdot \frac{1}{4}n^{8\varepsilon}}
= -\frac{4C^2}{L M n^{8\varepsilon}}.
\]
Since \(L, M, n\) and \(n^{8\varepsilon}\) are all positive, \(C^2 / (LMn^{8\varepsilon})\) is non-negative.
Thus,
\[
-\frac{4C^2}{L M n^{8\varepsilon}} \le -\frac{C^2}{LMn^{8\varepsilon}}.
\]
\end{proof}
