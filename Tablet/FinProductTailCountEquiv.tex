\begin{helper}[FinProductTailCountEquiv]
\label{helper:FinProductTailCountEquiv}
Let \(K\subseteq \operatorname{Fin}(m)\times\operatorname{Fin}(m)\), let
\(T\in\mathbb R\), and use the standard equivalence
\(\operatorname{Fin}(m)\times\operatorname{Fin}(m)\simeq
\operatorname{Fin}(m^2)\).  The number of \(n\)-element subsets
\(S\subseteq\operatorname{Fin}(m)\times\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) with
\(|S\cap K|>T\) is equal to the indicator sum over \(n\)-element subsets of
\(\operatorname{Fin}(m^2)\) for the transported marked set.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The standard equivalence is a bijection on the ambient finite type.  Sending a
subset \(S\) to its image under this equivalence is therefore a bijection
between \(n\)-element subsets of
\(\operatorname{Fin}(m)\times\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) and \(n\)-element subsets
of \(\operatorname{Fin}(m^2)\).  It also sends \(S\cap K\) bijectively onto
the intersection of the transported subset with the transported marked set,
so the intersection cardinality is preserved.  Rewriting the cardinality of a
filtered family as the sum of its \(0/1\) indicator over all \(n\)-element
subsets gives the claimed equality.
\end{proof}
