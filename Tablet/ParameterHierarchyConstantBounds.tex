\begin{lemma}
Let \(\varepsilon>0\), and define
\[
\eta=\min\{\varepsilon/2,1/32\},
\]
\[
\varepsilon_1=
\frac12\min\left\{
\eta,\left(\frac{\eta^3}{32}\right)^2,\frac{\eta^3}{40}
\right\},
\]
and
\[
\varepsilon_2=\frac12\min\left\{\varepsilon_1,\frac{\varepsilon_1}{30},1\right\}.
\]
Then
\[
0<\eta<\varepsilon,\qquad \eta\le\varepsilon,\qquad
0<\varepsilon_2<\varepsilon_1<\eta<1,\qquad \eta<\frac1{16},
\]
and
\[
3\varepsilon_2\le\frac{\varepsilon_1}{10},\qquad
8\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}+10\varepsilon_1\le\frac{\eta^3}{2}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Since \(\varepsilon>0\), both \(\varepsilon/2\) and \(1/32\) are positive.
Therefore their minimum \(\eta\) is positive.  Also
\[
\eta\le\varepsilon/2<\varepsilon,
\]
so \(0<\eta<\varepsilon\), and hence \(\eta\le\varepsilon\).  The other branch
of the minimum gives
\[
\eta\le 1/32<1/16<1.
\]

The three entries in the minimum defining \(\varepsilon_1\) are positive:
\(\eta>0\), \((\eta^3/32)^2>0\), and \(\eta^3/40>0\).  Hence their minimum is
positive, and multiplying by \(1/2\) gives \(0<\varepsilon_1\).  Because that
minimum is at most \(\eta\),
\[
\varepsilon_1\le\frac12\eta<\eta.
\]

Similarly the three entries in the minimum defining \(\varepsilon_2\) are
positive: \(\varepsilon_1>0\), \(\varepsilon_1/30>0\), and \(1>0\).  Hence
\(0<\varepsilon_2\).  Since the minimum is at most \(\varepsilon_1\),
\[
\varepsilon_2\le\frac12\varepsilon_1<\varepsilon_1.
\]
Since the same minimum is at most \(\varepsilon_1/30\),
\[
\varepsilon_2\le\frac12\cdot\frac{\varepsilon_1}{30}
=\frac{\varepsilon_1}{60}.
\]
Multiplying by \(3\) gives
\[
3\varepsilon_2\le\frac{\varepsilon_1}{20}\le\frac{\varepsilon_1}{10}.
\]

It remains to prove the square-root bound.  From the second entry in the
minimum defining \(\varepsilon_1\),
\[
\varepsilon_1\le \left(\frac{\eta^3}{32}\right)^2.
\]
Both sides are nonnegative, so monotonicity of the square root gives
\[
\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}\le \frac{\eta^3}{32}.
\]
Thus
\[
8\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}\le \frac{\eta^3}{4}.
\]
From the third entry in the same minimum,
\[
\varepsilon_1\le\frac{\eta^3}{40},
\]
so
\[
10\varepsilon_1\le\frac{\eta^3}{4}.
\]
Adding the two estimates yields
\[
8\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}+10\varepsilon_1
\le \frac{\eta^3}{4}+\frac{\eta^3}{4}
=\frac{\eta^3}{2}.
\]
\end{proof}
