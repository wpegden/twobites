\begin{helper}
Let \(\eta\in\mathbb R\) and \(n\in\mathbb N\).  Put
\[
L=\log n,\quad
k_R=(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL},\quad
K=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalIndependenceScale}(1+\eta,n),\quad
k=(K:\mathbb R),
\]
and
\[
p=\frac12\sqrt{\frac Ln}.
\]
Assume \(0<\eta\), \(0<n\), \(0<L\), \(k_R\le k<2k_R\), and
\[
\frac{8\log(2(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL})}
{(1+\eta)^2\sqrt n\,L^{3/2}}
\le\frac{\eta^3}{2}.
\]
Then
\[
4\log k\le\frac{\eta^3}{2}pk^2.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(0<\eta\), \(1+\eta>0\), and hence \(k_R>0\).  From
\(k_R\le k\) we have \(k>0\), and from \(k<2k_R\) the monotonicity of
\(\log\) gives
\[
\log k\le \log(2k_R)
=\log(2(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL}).
\]
The displayed threshold, multiplied by the positive denominator, gives
\[
8\log(2(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL})
\le \frac{\eta^3}{2}(1+\eta)^2\sqrt n\,L^{3/2}.
\]
Dividing by \(2\) yields the corresponding bound with the coefficient \(4\).
Using
\[
pk_R^2
=\frac12\sqrt{\frac Ln}\,(1+\eta)^2 nL
=\frac{(1+\eta)^2}{2}\sqrt n\,L^{3/2},
\]
this becomes
\[
4\log(2(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL})
\le \frac{\eta^3}{2}p k_R^2.
\]
Finally, \(p\ge0\) and \(0\le k_R\le k\), so \(p k_R^2\le p k^2\).
Combining these inequalities proves the claim.
\end{proof}
