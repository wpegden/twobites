\begin{helper}
For every two-bite sample \(\omega\), the blue part of
\(\noderef{TwoBiteFinalGraph}\) contains no triangle.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let \(G_B\) be the final blue graph and \(\widetilde G_B\) the blue overlay
graph.  Suppose, for contradiction, that \(u,v,w\) span a triangle in \(G_B\).
Then all three edges are blue overlay edges that survived the blue deletion
predicate.

Write \(b_x\) for the blue projection of \(x\).  Since each blue overlay edge
\(xy\) comes from blue base adjacency \(b_xb_y\), and the blue base graph is
simple and loopless, the three projected vertices \(b_u,b_v,b_w\) are pairwise
distinct.  Applying \(\noderef{FinPairRankLatestOfDistinct}\) to these three
blue projections, one of the three projected blue pairs has pair-rank strictly
larger than the other two.

If the largest rank is that of \(uv\), then the blue deletion predicate for
the directed edge \(uv\) holds with witness \(w\), using the three blue overlay
edges and the strict inequalities asserting that the other two projected-pair
ranks are smaller.  Therefore \(uv\) is blue-deleted, contradicting that it is
an edge of \(G_B\).  The same argument with the vertex names permuted handles
the possibilities that \(uw\) or \(vw\) has the largest blue projected-pair
rank.  Hence no blue triangle remains.
\end{proof}
