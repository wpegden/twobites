\begin{helper}[Natural reduced vertex times Gaussian log tail is negligible]
For every constant \(c>0\) and
\(m(n)=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n)\),
\[
  m(n)\exp\!\left(-c(\log n)^2\right)\to 0 .
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
It is enough to compare \(m(n)\) with \(n\).  Unfolding
\(\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}\),
\(\noderef{TwoBiteReducedVertexCount}\), and \(\noderef{TwoBiteStretch}\),
we have \(m(n)\le n/(\log n)^2\).  For sufficiently large \(n\),
\(\log n\ge1\), so \(m(n)\le n\).  Therefore
\[
  m(n)e^{-c(\log n)^2}
  \le n e^{-c(\log n)^2}
  = \exp(\log n-c(\log n)^2).
\]
As \(y\to\infty\), \(y-cy^2\to-\infty\), hence
\(\exp(y-cy^2)\to0\).  Composing with \(y=\log n\to\infty\) and squeezing
between \(0\) and the displayed upper bound proves the claim.
\end{proof}
