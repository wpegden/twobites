\begin{helper}[GnpVertexDegreeMarkovTailBounds]
Let \(m\in\mathbb N\), let \(0\le p\le1\), let
\(r\in\operatorname{Fin}(m)\), and let \(t\ge0\).  For every real threshold
\(A\), the \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}\)-mass of the upper and lower degree
tails of \(\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)\) is bounded by the corresponding
exponential moments:
\[
\sum_G {\bf 1}_{A\le \noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)}
  \noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G)
\le
e^{-tA}\sum_G e^{t\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)}
  \noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G),
\]
and
\[
\sum_G {\bf 1}_{\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)\le A}
  \noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G)
\le
e^{tA}\sum_G e^{-t\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)}
  \noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The assumptions \(0\le p\le1\) make every factor in
\(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}\) nonnegative, hence every graph weight is
nonnegative.  For the upper tail, if
\(A\le D=\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)\), then
\[
1\le e^{t(D-A)}=e^{-tA}e^{tD}
\]
because \(t(D-A)\ge0\).  Multiplying by the nonnegative graph weight and
summing over \(G\) gives the first inequality; graphs outside the event
contribute \(0\) on the left and a nonnegative term on the right.  The lower
tail is identical: if \(D\le A\), then
\[
1\le e^{t(A-D)}=e^{tA}e^{-tD},
\]
and summing the resulting pointwise inequality gives the second bound.
\end{proof}
