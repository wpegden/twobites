\begin{helper}
Let \(H\) be a finite family of subsets \(A_x\subseteq I\), let \(f\) be a
projection map, and put
\[
S=\sum_{x\in H}|A_x|.
\]
Assume \(0\le\delta\).  Assume the pairwise-overlap loss is at most
\(\delta S\),
\[
\binom{|H|}{2}_{\mathbb R} C\le \delta S,
\]
and that the image of the union is also at most \(\delta S\),
\[
\left|f\!\left(\bigcup_{x\in H}A_x\right)\right|\le\delta S.
\]
If every pairwise intersection \(A_x\cap A_y\), \(x\ne y\), has size at most
\(C\), then
\[
(1-2\delta)S\le |I|-\left|f(I)\right|.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Write \(W=\bigcup_{x\in H}A_x\).  Since every \(A_x\subseteq I\), also
\(W\subseteq I\).  The finite union lower bound with pairwise overlap cap
\(C\) gives
\[
S-\binom{|H|}{2}_{\mathbb R}C\le |W|.
\]
By the assumed overlap-loss estimate, this implies
\[
(1-\delta)S\le |W|.
\]
The assumed image-loss estimate gives
\[
|f(W)|\le\delta S.
\]
Therefore
\[
(1-2\delta)S\le |W|-|f(W)|.
\]
Finally, since \(W\subseteq I\), the projection deficit cannot decrease when
we pass from \(W\) to \(I\):
\[
|W|-|f(W)|\le |I|-|f(I)|.
\]
Combining the last two inequalities proves the claim.
\end{proof}
