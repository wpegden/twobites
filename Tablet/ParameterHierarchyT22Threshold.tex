\begin{helper}
For every \(\eta>0\) with \(\eta<1/16\), there is \(n_0\) such that for
every \(n>n_0\), if \(L=\log n\), then
\[
\frac{2(\log L)\sqrt L}{n^{1/4-\eta}}<\frac12
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\frac{\log L}{L}<\frac12.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Put \(r=1/4-\eta\).  Since \(\eta<1/16\), we have \(r>0\).  The standard
fact \(\log n=o(n^{r/2})\) lets us enlarge \(n_0\) so that
\[
\log n\le \frac14 n^{r/2}.
\]
After enlarging \(n_0\) further, we may also assume \(L=\log n\ge1\) and
\(\log L\le \frac14 L\).  Then \(\log L\le L\) and \(\sqrt L\le L\), so
\[
2(\log L)\sqrt L\le 2L^2
\le 2\left(\frac14 n^{r/2}\right)^2
=\frac18 n^r<\frac12 n^r.
\]
Dividing by the positive number \(n^r\) proves the first inequality.  The
second inequality follows by dividing \(\log L\le L/4<L/2\) by the positive
number \(L\).
\end{proof}
