\begin{helper}
For every two-bite sample \(\omega\), the final graph has no mixed triangle in
which two red edges share a vertex and the opposite edge is blue.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Suppose, for contradiction, that \(uv\) and \(uw\) are final red edges and
\(vw\) is a final blue edge.  A final red edge is an overlay red edge that was
not deleted, so \(uv\) and \(uw\) are red overlay edges.  A final blue edge is
an overlay blue edge that was not deleted, so \(vw\) is a blue overlay edge.

The blue mixed-deletion clause in \(\noderef{TwoBiteFinalGraph}\) says that if
there is a center \(c\) such that \(cv\) and \(cw\) are red overlay edges while
\(vw\) is a blue overlay edge, then the blue edge \(vw\) is selected for
deletion.  Applying this clause with center \(c=u\), the three overlay edges
\(uv,uw,vw\) witness that \(vw\) is blue-deleted.  This contradicts the fact
that \(vw\) remains a final blue edge.
\end{proof}
