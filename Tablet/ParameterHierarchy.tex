\begin{definition}
For the two-bite proof, a parameter hierarchy for a fixed error
\(\varepsilon>0\) consists of fixed constants
\(\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2\) and a threshold \(n_0=n_0(\varepsilon_2)\) with
\[
0<\varepsilon_2<\varepsilon_1<\varepsilon<1,
\qquad
\varepsilon<\frac1{16},
\qquad
3\varepsilon_2\le \frac{\varepsilon_1}{10},
\]
\[
8\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}+10\varepsilon_1\le \frac{\varepsilon^3}{2},
\qquad
n_0\ge \varepsilon_2^{-2}.
\]
The same threshold \(n_0\) also satisfies the finite list of large-\(n\)
analytic inequalities in
\(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyEventualComparisons}\).
\end{definition}
