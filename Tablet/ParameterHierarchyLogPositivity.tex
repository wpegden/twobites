\begin{helper}
Assume \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyEventualComparisons}(\varepsilon,\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2,n_0)\) and let \(n>n_0\). Then
\[
\log n > 0.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The first line of \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyEventualComparisons}\) gives
\[
0 < M
\]
and
\[
1 \le 2pM,
\]
where \(M=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n)\) and \(p=\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}(1/2,n)=\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\frac{\log n}{n}}\). Because \(M>0\), the inequality \(1 \le 2pM\) implies \(p>0\). Since \(p>0\), the term inside the square root must be strictly positive, meaning
\[
\frac{\log n}{n} > 0.
\]
Because \(n>0\) (otherwise the fraction would be zero), we must have \(\log n > 0\).
\end{proof}
