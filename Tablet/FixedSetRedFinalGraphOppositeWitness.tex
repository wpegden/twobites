\begin{lemma}
Let \(q\) be a red projected coordinate in the staged-open set for \(I\).
If the red base pair \(q\) is present and the final shadow graph is
independent on \(I\), then there is a blue base vertex \(b\) such that
\(q\) is a red projected pair from
\(X(\operatorname{blue}(b))\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unpack staged-openness. The coordinate \(q\) is represented by two
endpoints \(u,v\in I\), and it is not same-color closed. The present red
base edge gives a red overlay edge between \(u\) and \(v\).

If this overlay edge survived in the final shadow graph, it would be a
final shadow edge inside \(I\), contradicting independence. Therefore the
red deletion alternative in \(\noderef{TwoBiteFinalGraph}\) holds.

If the deletion is caused by a red triangle, the rank comparisons say that
the third red coordinate is lower than both projected endpoints by
\(\noderef{FinPairRankThirdLessOfLatest}\). Thus both endpoints lie in the
corresponding \(X^+\)-fiber, making \(q\) same-color closed, contrary to
staged-openness. Hence the only remaining deletion alternative is the
mixed one: there is a blue center adjacent in blue to both endpoints.
Taking \(b\) to be its blue projection places \(u\) and \(v\) in
\(X(\operatorname{blue}(b))\), so \(q\) is the required opposite-color
projected pair.
\end{proof}
