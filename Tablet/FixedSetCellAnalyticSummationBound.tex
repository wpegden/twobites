\begin{helper}
For every natural number \(k\) and real numbers \(p,\varepsilon_1,f\),
\[
k^4\exp\!\left(
8\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}\,p k^2
-p(f-10\varepsilon_1 k^2)\right)
\le
\exp\!\left(
-pf+8\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}\,p k^2
+10\varepsilon_1 p k^2+4\log k\right).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
If \(k=0\), the left-hand side is \(0\), while the right-hand side is a
positive exponential, so the inequality is immediate.  Otherwise \(k>0\).
Then \(k=\exp(\log k)\), hence \(k^4=\exp(4\log k)\).  Multiplying the two
exponentials on the left and using \(\exp(x)\exp(y)=\exp(x+y)\), the left-hand
side becomes
\[
\exp\!\left(
4\log k+8\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}\,p k^2
-p(f-10\varepsilon_1k^2)\right).
\]
Expanding the final product in the exponent gives exactly
\[
\exp\!\left(
-pf+8\sqrt{\varepsilon_1}\,p k^2
+10\varepsilon_1 p k^2+4\log k\right),
\]
which is the right-hand side.  Thus the desired inequality follows by equality
in the positive-\(k\) case and by positivity of the exponential in the zero
case.
\end{proof}
