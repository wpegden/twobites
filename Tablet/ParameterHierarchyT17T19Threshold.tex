\begin{helper}
For every \(\eta>0\) and \(\varepsilon_2>0\), there is \(n_0\) such that
for every \(n>n_0\), if \(L=\log n\), then
\[
\frac{100\cdot5(1+\eta)(\log L)^2L^3}{\sqrt{nL}}
\le\frac{\varepsilon_2}{100},
\]
\[
\frac{200\cdot5(1+\eta)(\log L)^2L^3}{\sqrt{nL}}
\le\frac{\varepsilon_2}{100},
\]
and
\[
\frac{400\cdot5(1+\eta)(\log L)^2L^5}{\sqrt{nL}}
\le\frac{\varepsilon_2}{100}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
It is enough to prove the last displayed inequality eventually, with
\(L\ge1\) and \(\log L\ge0\), because for \(L\ge1\) the first two left
sides are bounded above by the third one: \(100L^3\le400L^5\) and
\(200L^3\le400L^5\), while the common factor
\(5(1+\eta)(\log L)^2/\sqrt{nL}\) is nonnegative.

Let \(C=400\cdot5(1+\eta)\).  Since \(\log n=o(n^{1/16})\), choose
\(n_0\) large enough that for every \(n>n_0\),
\[
L\ge1,\qquad \log L\ge0,\qquad L\le n^{1/16},
\qquad n^{-1/16}\le\frac{\varepsilon_2}{100C}.
\]
Then
\[
\frac{L^7}{\sqrt n}
\le \frac{n^{7/16}}{n^{1/2}}
=n^{-1/16}
\le\frac{\varepsilon_2}{100C},
\]
and hence
\[
C\frac{L^7}{\sqrt n}\le\frac{\varepsilon_2}{100}.
\]
For such \(n\), \(\log L\le L\), so \((\log L)^2\le L^2\), and
\(\sqrt{nL}\ge\sqrt n\) because \(L\ge1\).  Therefore
\[
\frac{400\cdot5(1+\eta)(\log L)^2L^5}{\sqrt{nL}}
\le
400\cdot5(1+\eta)\frac{L^7}{\sqrt n}
\le\frac{\varepsilon_2}{100}.
\]
As noted at the start, this also gives the two smaller displayed bounds.
\end{proof}
