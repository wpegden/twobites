\begin{helper}
For every \(\eta>0\) and \(\varepsilon_1>0\), there is \(n_0\) such that
for every \(n>n_0\), if \(L=\log n\), then
\[
\frac{2\log L}{(1+\eta)L^4}\le\varepsilon_1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(L=\log n\to\infty\), choose \(n_0\) so that for every \(n>n_0\),
\[
L\ge 1,\qquad L\ge \frac{2}{(1+\eta)\varepsilon_1}.
\]
For \(L\ge1\), the elementary inequality \(\log L\le L\) gives
\[
\frac{2\log L}{(1+\eta)L^4}
\le \frac{2L}{(1+\eta)L^4}
=\frac{2}{(1+\eta)L^3}
\le\frac{2}{(1+\eta)L}.
\]
The second threshold condition says
\[
\frac{2}{(1+\eta)L}\le\varepsilon_1.
\]
Combining the inequalities proves the claim.
\end{proof}
