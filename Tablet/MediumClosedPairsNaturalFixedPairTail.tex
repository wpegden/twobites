\begin{lemma}
Fix \(n\) and \(\varepsilon\), and set
\[
K=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalIndependenceScale}(1+\varepsilon,n),
\qquad
p=\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}\!\left(\frac12,n\right),
\]
\[
A=\frac{K\,n^{1/4-\varepsilon}}{6(\log n)^2}.
\]
Assume
\[
A>0,\qquad p\ge0.
\]
Let \(B_R,B_B\) be the red and blue parts of a fixed base-vertex family and
let \(P_R,P_B\) be the corresponding red and blue projection sets.  Suppose
\[
|P_R|\le K,\qquad |P_B|\le K,
\]
\[
p(|B_R|+|B_B|)K\le \frac{A}{4e},
\qquad
\frac{A}{4}\ge n^{3/4-2\varepsilon}.
\]
If the fixed-pair bad event has probability, or product weight, at most
\[
\binom{|B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|}{\lceil A\rceil}
  p^{\lceil A\rceil},
\]
then it has probability, or product weight, at most
\[
\exp\!\left(-n^{3/4-2\varepsilon}\right).
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The assumptions \(A>0\) and \(p\ge0\), together with the first mean
inequality, are exactly the positivity and mean hypotheses needed by
\(\noderef{MediumClosedPairsFixedPairCandidateTail}\), with candidate count
bounded by
\[
|B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|\le (|B_R|+|B_B|)K.
\]
Applying that helper gives the fixed-pair bound
\[
P\le \exp(-A/4).
\]
The second displayed hypothesis gives
\[
-A/4\le -n^{3/4-2\varepsilon}.
\]
Since the exponential function is monotone, this implies
\[
\exp(-A/4)\le \exp(-n^{3/4-2\varepsilon}),
\]
and the claimed natural-parameter tail bound follows.
\end{proof}
