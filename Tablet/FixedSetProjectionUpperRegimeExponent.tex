\begin{lemma}
Assume \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchy}(\varepsilon,\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2,n_0)\),
\(\eta>0\), and \(\eta\le\varepsilon^2/320\).  For all sufficiently large
\(n\), with
\[
K=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalIndependenceScale}(1+\varepsilon,n),
\qquad
p=\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}\!\left(\frac12,n\right),
\]
the following holds uniformly for \(0\le\ell_R,\ell_B\le K\).  If
\[
x_R=\ell_R/K,\qquad x_B=\ell_B/K,\qquad
x_R+x_B\ge1+\varepsilon/2,
\]
then
\[
\begin{aligned}
&\left(\eta+\left(\frac12+\eta\right)-\frac{2-x_R-x_B}{2}\right)K\log n\\
&\quad
-p\left(
\binom{\ell_R}{2}_{\mathbb R}+\binom{\ell_B}{2}_{\mathbb R}
-\binom{K-\ell_R}{2}_{\mathbb R}
-\binom{K-\ell_B}{2}_{\mathbb R}
-\varepsilon^3K^2\right)\\
&\le
-\frac{\varepsilon(x_R+x_B-1-\varepsilon^2-\varepsilon^3)}{2}K\log n
+3\eta K\log n .
\end{aligned}
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Write \(s=x_R+x_B\) and \(u=s-1\).  Expanding
\(\binom y2_{\mathbb R}=y(y-1)/2\) gives, for any \(0\le x\le1\),
\[
\binom{xK}{2}_{\mathbb R}-\binom{K-xK}{2}_{\mathbb R}
=\frac{x^2K^2-xK-(1-x)^2K^2+(1-x)K}{2}
=\frac{(2x-1)(K^2-K)}{2}.
\]
Adding this identity for \(x=x_R\) and \(x=x_B\) gives the exact identity
\[
\binom{\ell_R}{2}_{\mathbb R}+\binom{\ell_B}{2}_{\mathbb R}
-\binom{K-\ell_R}{2}_{\mathbb R}
-\binom{K-\ell_B}{2}_{\mathbb R}
=u(K^2-K). \tag{1}
\]
Thus the left side in the lemma is
\[
\left(2\eta+\frac{u}{2}\right)K\log n
-p\bigl(u(K^2-K)-\varepsilon^3K^2\bigr). \tag{2}
\]

The rounded comparison in \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyEventualComparisons}\)
gives
\[
(1+\varepsilon)\sqrt{n\log n}\le K
<(1+\varepsilon)\sqrt{n\log n}+1.
\]
Since \(p=\frac12\sqrt{\log n/n}\), increasing the threshold makes the two
uniform estimates
\[
\left|pK-\frac{1+\varepsilon}{2}\log n\right|
\le \frac{\eta}{16}\log n,
\qquad
p\le \frac{\eta}{16}\log n
\tag{3}
\]
valid.  The second estimate is trivial for large \(n\), and the first follows
by multiplying the displayed bound on \(K\) by \(p\), where the additive error is
exactly \(p\).  We also increase the threshold so that \(K\log n\ge0\).

The regime hypotheses give \(0\le u\le1\).  From (3),
\[
p\,u(K^2-K)
\ge
\left(\frac{1+\varepsilon}{2}\log n-\frac{\eta}{16}\log n\right)uK
-\frac{\eta}{16}K\log n,
\]
and
\[
p\varepsilon^3K^2
\le
\left(\frac{1+\varepsilon}{2}\log n+\frac{\eta}{16}\log n\right)
\varepsilon^3K.
\]
Substituting these two estimates into (2) yields
\[
\left(
2\eta+\frac{u}{2}
-\frac{1+\varepsilon}{2}u
+\frac{1+\varepsilon}{2}\varepsilon^3
\right)K\log n
+\left(\frac{u}{16}+\frac1{16}+\frac{\varepsilon^3}{16}\right)\eta K\log n.
\]
Since \(0\le u\le1\) and \(0<\varepsilon<1\), the parenthesized error
coefficient is at most \(3/16\).  The non-error coefficient is
\[
-\frac{\varepsilon(u-\varepsilon^2-\varepsilon^3)}{2},
\]
plus the explicit \(2\eta\) from the first line.  Therefore the total error is
at most \((2+3/16)\eta K\log n\), which is bounded by \(3\eta K\log n\).
Substituting \(u=s-1=x_R+x_B-1\) proves the claimed bound.
\end{proof}
