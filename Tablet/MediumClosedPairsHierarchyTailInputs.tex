\begin{lemma}
Assume \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchy}(\varepsilon,\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2,n_0)\).
Then, for all sufficiently large \(n\), the following holds.  Let
\[
K=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalIndependenceScale}(1+\varepsilon,n),\qquad
p=\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}\!\left(\frac12,n\right),
\]
and
\[
A=\frac{K\,n^{1/4-\varepsilon}}{6(\log n)^2}.
\]
For every fixed split \(B_R,B_B\) satisfying
\[
|B_R|+|B_B|
\le \frac32 K n^{1/4-3\varepsilon},
\]
we have \(A>0\), \(p\ge0\),
\[
p(|B_R|+|B_B|)K\le\frac{A}{4e},
\]
and
\[
n^{3/4-2\varepsilon}\le \frac A4.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The hierarchy gives \(0<\varepsilon<1/16\) and hence \(0<\varepsilon<1\).
Use the eventual base-threshold package
\(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyBaseThreshold}\) and the medium exponential
threshold package \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyT28Threshold}\), and enlarge
the threshold so that \(n>n_0\) and both packages apply.

For such \(n\), \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyLogPositivity}\) gives
\(\log n>0\), and of course \(n>0\).  The base-threshold package gives
\[
(1+\varepsilon)\sqrt{n\log n}\le K
\quad\text{and}\quad
K<2(1+\varepsilon)\sqrt{n\log n}.
\]
Since \(p=\frac12\sqrt{\log n/n}\), the upper bound on \(K\) gives
\[
pK\le
\frac12\sqrt{\frac{\log n}{n}}\,
2(1+\varepsilon)\sqrt{n\log n}
=(1+\varepsilon)\log n.
\]

The strengthened absorption helper
\(\noderef{MediumClosedPairsCompressedTailAbsorptions}\), applied with this
positive \(\varepsilon\), is an eventual statement.  Intersecting that
eventual statement with the current base and \(T28\) thresholds, we are
working only with \(n\) for which it gives
\[
36e(1+\varepsilon)(\log n)^3\le n^{2\varepsilon}
\quad\text{and}\quad
24(\log n)^2\le(1+\varepsilon)\sqrt{\log n}\,n^\varepsilon.
\]
The hypotheses just proved are exactly the positivity, mean, and lower
threshold hypotheses required by
\(\noderef{MediumClosedPairsNaturalTailHypotheses}\), which supplies the four
claimed conclusions.
\end{proof}
