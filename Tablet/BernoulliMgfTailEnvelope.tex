\begin{helper}[BernoulliMgfTailEnvelope]
Let \(m\in\mathbb N\), \(0\le p\le1\), and \(\epsilon,t\in\mathbb R\).  Then
the two-tail moment expression
\[
m\left[
\exp(-t(1+\epsilon)pm)(1-p+p\exp t)^{m-1}
+
\exp(t(1-\epsilon)pm)(1-p+p\exp(-t))^{m-1}
\right]
\]
is at most
\[
m\left[
\exp\!\left(-t(1+\epsilon)pm+(m-1)p(\exp t-1)\right)
+
\exp\!\left(t(1-\epsilon)pm+(m-1)p(\exp(-t)-1)\right)
\right].
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
For any real \(u\), the factor \(1-p+p\exp u\) is nonnegative because it is
\((1-p)+p\exp u\) with \(0\le p\le1\).  Also
\[
1-p+p\exp u = 1+p(\exp u-1)\le \exp(p(\exp u-1))
\]
by the elementary inequality \(1+x\le\exp x\).  Raising both sides to the
\((m-1)\)-st power gives
\[
(1-p+p\exp u)^{m-1}
\le
\exp\!\left((m-1)p(\exp u-1)\right).
\]
Apply this estimate with \(u=t\) to the upper-tail factor and with \(u=-t\)
to the lower-tail factor.  Multiplying by the positive exponential prefactors,
using \(\exp a\,\exp b=\exp(a+b)\), adding the two inequalities, and finally
multiplying by the nonnegative number \(m\) gives the result.
\end{proof}
