\begin{helper}
For every \(m,n\), every \(K\subseteq \operatorname{Fin}(m)\times
\operatorname{Fin}(m)\), and every real threshold \(T\), the number of
\(n\)-element subsets \(S\subseteq \operatorname{Fin}(m)\times
\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) satisfying
\[
|S\cap K|<T
\]
is equal to the corresponding lower-tail count after transporting
\(\operatorname{Fin}(m)\times\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) across the standard
equivalence with \(\operatorname{Fin}(m^2)\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let \(e:\operatorname{Fin}(m)\times\operatorname{Fin}(m)\simeq
\operatorname{Fin}(m^2)\) be the standard equivalence.  The map
\(S\mapsto e(S)\) is a bijection from \(n\)-element subsets of
\(\operatorname{Fin}(m)\times\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) to \(n\)-element subsets
of \(\operatorname{Fin}(m^2)\).  Its inverse is \(S'\mapsto e^{-1}(S')\), and
cardinality is preserved because \(e\) and \(e^{-1}\) are injective.

For every subset \(S\), the image of the intersection is the intersection of
the images:
\[
e(S\cap K)=e(S)\cap e(K).
\]
Injectivity of \(e\) preserves the cardinality of this intersection, so
\[
|S\cap K|=|e(S)\cap e(K)|.
\]
Hence the predicate \(|S\cap K|<T\) is carried exactly to the predicate
\(|S'\cap e(K)|<T\).  Summing the indicator of this predicate over the
\(n\)-element subsets of \(\operatorname{Fin}(m^2)\) therefore gives exactly
the original lower-tail count.
\end{proof}
