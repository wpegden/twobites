\begin{lemma}
Let \(L,N,M,T,V,q\) be real numbers with \(L>0\) and \(N>0\).  Suppose
\[
  \frac{N}{2L^2}\le M,\qquad 100L^3\le T,\qquad Vq\le LN .
\]
Then
\[
  \frac{eVq}{TM}\le \frac e{50}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The lower bounds on \(M\) and \(T\) give
\[
  TM\ge 100L^3\cdot \frac{N}{2L^2}=50LN .
\]
Since \(L,N>0\), the denominator \(50LN\) is positive.  Also
\[
  eVq\le eLN .
\]
Dividing the numerator by the larger denominator \(TM\) can only decrease
the quotient, so
\[
  \frac{eVq}{TM}\le \frac{eLN}{50LN}=\frac e{50}.
\]
\end{proof}
