\begin{lemma}
For any natural numbers \(S,h,q,m\) with \(h\le q\le m\) and \(h\le S\),
we have the ratio equality
\[
\frac{(S)_h (m-h)_{q-h}}{(m)_q} = \prod_{\ell=0}^{h-1} \frac{S-\ell}{m-\ell}
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Since \(h\le q\le m\), the falling factorial in the denominator factors as
\[
(m)_q=(m)_h(m-h)_{q-h}.
\]
After substituting this factorization, the common nonzero factor
\((m-h)_{q-h}\) cancels.  The remaining quotient is
\[
\frac{(S)_h}{(m)_h}.
\]
The hypotheses \(h\le S\) and \(h\le m\) allow the falling factorials
\((S)_h\) and \((m)_h\) to be expanded without truncation:
\[
(S)_h=\prod_{\ell=0}^{h-1}(S-\ell),\qquad
(m)_h=\prod_{\ell=0}^{h-1}(m-\ell).
\]
Dividing the two products term by term gives
\[
\frac{(S)_h}{(m)_h}
=\prod_{\ell=0}^{h-1}\frac{S-\ell}{m-\ell},
\]
which is the claimed equality.
\end{proof}
