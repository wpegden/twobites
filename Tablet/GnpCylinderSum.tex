\begin{helper}[GnpCylinderSum]
\label{helper:GnpCylinderSum}
Let \(m\) be a natural number and \(p \in \mathbb{R}\).
Let \(r\) and \(s\) be distinct vertices in \(\operatorname{Fin}(m)\).
Let \(S\) be a subset of \(\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) such that \(r \notin S\) and \(s \notin S\).
The sum of the \(G(m,p)\) product law over graphs \(G\) where \(r\) and \(s\) are adjacent to all elements in \(S\) is exactly \((p^2)^{|S|}\):
\[
\sum_{G : \forall t \in S, G.Adj(r,t) \land G.Adj(s,t)} \noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p, G) = (p^2)^{|S|}.
\]
\end{helper}

\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeightSumOne}\), the graph weight is a product over all unordered pairs of vertices.
Fixing the adjacency of \(r\) and \(s\) to all vertices in \(S\) forces exactly \(2 |S|\) edge coordinates to be present, because \(r\) and \(s\) are distinct and disjoint from \(S\).
These forced edges contribute a factor of \(p^{2|S|} = (p^2)^{|S|}\).
Summing over the remaining unforced edge coordinates yields \(1\).
\end{proof}
