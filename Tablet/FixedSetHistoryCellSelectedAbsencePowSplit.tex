\begin{helper}
Let \(A\) be a finite absent-coordinate set and let \(Z\subseteq A\) be the
selected absent coordinates.  For every real \(p\),
\[
(1-p)^{|A|}
  =
(1-p)^{|A|-|Z|}(1-p)^{|Z|}.
\]
Thus the selected absent coordinates can be charged once as a separate
\((1-p)^{|Z|}\)-factor, with the unselected absent coordinates left in the
residual cylinder.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(Z\subseteq A\), finite cardinalities give
\[
|A|=(|A|-|Z|)+|Z|.
\]
Applying the ordinary power-addition identity to the base \(1-p\) yields the
displayed factorization.
\end{proof}
