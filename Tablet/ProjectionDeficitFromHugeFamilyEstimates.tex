\begin{helper}
Let \(H\) be a finite family of subsets \(A_x\subseteq I\), let \(f\) be a
projection map, and set
\[
S=\sum_{x\in H}|A_x|.
\]
Assume \(0\le\delta\) and \(0\le C\).  Assume each member has size at least
\(t\), the family size satisfies
\[
|H|\le 2|I|/t,
\]
all pairwise intersections \(A_x\cap A_y\) have size at most \(C\), and
\[
(2|I|/t+1)C\le\delta t.
\]
Assume also that every projected piece satisfies
\[
|f(A_x)|\le\delta |A_x|.
\]
Then
\[
(1-2\delta)S\le |I|-|f(I)|.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The lower size bound gives \( |H|t\le S\).  Since
\(|H|\le2|I|/t\), the elementary inequality
\[
\binom{|H|}{2}_{\mathbb R}\le |H|(2|I|/t+1)
\]
implies
\[
\binom{|H|}{2}_{\mathbb R}C
\le |H|(2|I|/t+1)C
\le |H|\delta t
\le \delta S.
\]
Here \(0\le C\) justifies multiplying the binomial bound by \(C\), and
\(0\le\delta\) justifies multiplying \(|H|t\le S\) by \(\delta\).
The pointwise image estimates give
\[
\sum_{x\in H}|f(A_x)|\le \delta S.
\]
The image of the union is contained in the union of the images, so by the
finite union bound
\[
\left|f\!\left(\bigcup_{x\in H}A_x\right)\right|
\le \sum_{x\in H}|f(A_x)|
\le\delta S.
\]
Thus the hypotheses of \(\noderef{ProjectionDeficitFromUnionLoss}\) are
satisfied: the pairwise-overlap loss is at most \(\delta S\), and the image of
the union is at most \(\delta S\).  Applying that helper gives
\[
(1-2\delta)S\le |I|-|f(I)|,
\]
as claimed.
\end{proof}
