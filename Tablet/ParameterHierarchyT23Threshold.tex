\begin{helper}
For every \(\eta>0\), there is \(n_0\) such that for every \(n>n_0\), if
\(L=\log n\), then
\[
\frac{200L^3}{n^\eta}\le1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(\log n=o(n^{\eta/3})\), enlarge \(n_0\) so that
\[
L=\log n\le \frac18 n^{\eta/3}
\]
for every \(n>n_0\).  Then
\[
L^3\le \frac1{512}n^\eta,
\]
and hence
\[
200L^3\le \frac{25}{64}n^\eta\le n^\eta.
\]
Because \(n^\eta>0\) for \(n>0\), division by \(n^\eta\) gives
\[
\frac{200L^3}{n^\eta}\le1.
\]
\end{proof}
