\begin{helper}
Let \(U\) be a finite set and let \(P,Z\subseteq U\) be disjoint finite
subsets.  For any real \(p\),
\[
\sum_{\substack{A\subseteq U\\ P\subseteq A,\ A\cap Z=\emptyset}}
  p^{|A|}(1-p)^{|U|-|A|}
=p^{|P|}(1-p)^{|Z|}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Write \(F=U\setminus(P\cup Z)\).  Every subset \(A\subseteq U\) with
\(P\subseteq A\) and \(A\cap Z=\emptyset\) has a unique representation
\[
A=P\cup V,\qquad V\subseteq F.
\]
Conversely every \(V\subseteq F\) gives such an \(A\).  The disjointness of
\(P\), \(Z\), and \(V\) gives
\[
|A|=|P|+|V|,\qquad |U|-|A|=|Z|+|F|-|V|.
\]
Therefore the sum is
\[
p^{|P|}(1-p)^{|Z|}
\sum_{V\subseteq F}p^{|V|}(1-p)^{|F|-|V|}.
\]
The remaining sum is the binomial expansion of
\((p+(1-p))^{|F|}=1\).  This proves the formula.
\end{proof}
