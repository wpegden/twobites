\begin{lemma}
Fix a finite set \(C\) of tagged red-or-blue endpoint-pair coordinates, that
is, elements of
\[
\operatorname{Sum}(\mathrm{Fin}(m)\times\mathrm{Fin}(m),
  \mathrm{Fin}(m)\times\mathrm{Fin}(m)).
\]
No increasing, nonloop, normalized, or terminal-universe condition is imposed
on the elements of \(C\).  Let \(t\) be an integer.  Suppose \(|C|\le N\),
\(0\le p\le1\), and suppose that every \(t\)-element set \(A\subseteq C\)
satisfies
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}
\left(\{\omega:\text{every coordinate in }A\text{ is present in }\omega\}\right)
\le p^t .
\]
Then
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}
\left(\{\omega:\text{at least }t\text{ coordinates of }C
\text{ are present in }\omega\}\right)
\le \binom Nt p^t .
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For a sample \(\omega\), let \(P(\omega)\subseteq C\) be the subset of
coordinates that are present in \(\omega\), using
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeCoordinateValue}\).  If \(|P(\omega)|\ge t\), then
finite subset selection gives a \(t\)-element subset \(A\subseteq P(\omega)\).
This \(A\) is also a \(t\)-element subset of \(C\), and every coordinate in
\(A\) is present in \(\omega\).  Hence the event
\[
\{|P(\omega)|\ge t\}
\]
is contained in the union, over all \(A\in\binom{C}{t}\), of the fixed-subset
events that all coordinates of \(A\) are present.

By \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbabilityUnionBound}\), using the nonnegative
sample weights supplied by \(0\le p\le1\), the probability of this union is at
most the sum of the probabilities of those fixed-subset events.  Each summand
is at most \(p^t\) by hypothesis, so the sum is at most
\[
|\binom{C}{t}|\,p^t=\binom{|C|}{t}p^t.
\]
Since \(|C|\le N\), monotonicity of binomial coefficients gives
\(\binom{|C|}{t}\le\binom Nt\).  Finally \(p^t\ge0\), so multiplying by
\(p^t\) preserves the inequality and yields the claimed bound.
\end{proof}
