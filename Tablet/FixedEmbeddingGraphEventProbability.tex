\begin{helper}[Fixed Embedding Graph Event Probability]
Fix \(n,m\in\mathbb N\), \(p\in\mathbb R\), an embedding
\(\pi:\mathrm{Fin}(n)\hookrightarrow \mathrm{Fin}(m)\times\mathrm{Fin}(m)\),
and a predicate \(K\) on ordered pairs of red and blue graphs on
\(\mathrm{Fin}(m)\).  Then
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m,p)
  \{\omega:\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}(\omega)=\pi
    \text{ and } K(\omega_R,\omega_B)\}
=
\noderef{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)
\sum_{(G_R,G_B):K(G_R,G_B)}
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_R)\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_B).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\) and
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}\).  A sample is a triple
\((G_R,G_B,\rho)\), and its weight is
\[
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_R)\,
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_B)\,
\noderef{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m).
\]
The event in question is the conjunction of \(\rho=\pi\) with the graph-pair
predicate \(K(G_R,G_B)\).

Rewrite the finite sum over samples as an iterated sum over \(G_R\), \(G_B\),
and \(\rho\).  For fixed \(G_R\) and \(G_B\), the inner sum over embeddings is
\[
\sum_\rho
\mathbf 1_{\rho=\pi\ \wedge\ K(G_R,G_B)}
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_R)
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_B)
\noderef{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m).
\]
If \(K(G_R,G_B)\) is false this inner sum is \(0\).  If \(K(G_R,G_B)\) is
true, exactly the single embedding \(\rho=\pi\) contributes, so the inner sum
is
\[
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_R)
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_B)
\noderef{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m).
\]
Thus the full sum is
\[
\sum_{(G_R,G_B):K(G_R,G_B)}
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_R)
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_B)
\noderef{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m),
\]
and the uniform injection factor is constant in the graph-pair sum, giving the
claimed identity.
\end{proof}
