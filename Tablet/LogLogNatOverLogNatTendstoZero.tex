\begin{helper}
As \(k\to\infty\) through the natural numbers,
\[
\frac{\log\log k}{\log k}\to0 .
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The real-variable estimate \(\log x=o(x)\) as \(x\to\infty\) gives
\[
\frac{\log x}{x}\to0 .
\]
Also \(\log k\to\infty\) as \(k\to\infty\).  Composing the real-variable
limit with \(x=\log k\) gives
\[
\frac{\log(\log k)}{\log k}\to0 .
\]
\end{proof}
