\begin{helper}
Let \(\kappa,S,\ell,k\) be real numbers with
\[
1\le \kappa,\qquad 0<S,\qquad 0<\ell,\qquad 1\le \ell,
\]
and suppose
\[
k<2\kappa S.
\]
Then
\[
\frac{2k}{S/\ell}+1\le 5\kappa\ell.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(S>0\) and \(\ell>0\), the denominator \(S/\ell\) is positive.  From
\(k<2\kappa S\), multiplying by \(2\) and dividing by \(S/\ell\) gives
\[
\frac{2k}{S/\ell}<4\kappa\ell,
\]
because \((S/\ell)^{-1}=\ell/S\).  Also \(1\le\kappa\) and \(1\le\ell\), with
both factors nonnegative, imply \(1\le\kappa\ell\).  Therefore
\[
\frac{2k}{S/\ell}+1
\le 4\kappa\ell+1
\le 4\kappa\ell+\kappa\ell
=5\kappa\ell.
\]
\end{proof}
