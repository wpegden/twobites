\begin{lemma}\label{ProjectionSizeAnalyticParameterBounds}
For any $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $n_0$ such that for all $n \ge n_0$, if $m$ is the natural reduced vertex count and $k$ is the natural independence scale for $1+\varepsilon$, then $n / (2 \log^2 n) \le m$, $k \le 2(1+\varepsilon)\sqrt{n \log n}$, $1 \le k \le n$, and $n \le m^2$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
This follows from the asymptotic growth rates of logarithms and polynomials, along with the parameter bounds in \(\noderef{ParameterHierarchyBaseThreshold}\).
\end{proof}
