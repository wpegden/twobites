\begin{helper}
For every \(\eta>0\), there is \(n_0\) such that for every \(n>n_0\), if
\(L=\log n\), then
\[
\frac{8\log(2(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL})}
{(1+\eta)^2\sqrt n\,L^{3/2}}
\le\frac{\eta^3}{2}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Put \(\kappa=1+\eta\) and
\[
\delta=\frac{\eta^3\kappa^2}{16}>0.
\]
Since \(\log n=o(n^{1/4})\), for all sufficiently large \(n\),
\[
2\log n\le n^{1/4}.
\]
Since \(n^{1/4}\to\infty\), after increasing the threshold also have
\[
1\le \delta n^{1/4}.
\]
Then \(n^{1/4}\le \delta\sqrt n\).  Also require \(n\ge1\), \(L\ge1\), and
\(2\kappa\le n\).  The inequality \(\log n\le n\) gives \(L\le n\), hence
\(\sqrt{nL}\le n\).  Therefore
\[
2(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL}=2\kappa\sqrt{nL}\le n^2,
\]
and by monotonicity of \(\log\),
\[
\log(2(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL})\le \log(n^2)=2\log n\le n^{1/4}
\le \delta\sqrt n.
\]
Because \(L\ge1\), also \(L^{3/2}\ge1\).  Multiplying the last displayed
inequality by \(8\) gives
\[
8\log(2(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL})
\le \frac{\eta^3}{2}(1+\eta)^2\sqrt n
\le \frac{\eta^3}{2}(1+\eta)^2\sqrt n\,L^{3/2}.
\]
The denominator in the statement is positive for these \(n\), so division by
it gives the required threshold.
\end{proof}
