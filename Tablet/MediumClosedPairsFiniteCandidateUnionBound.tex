\begin{helper}
Let \(n,m\in\mathbb N\), let \(p\in[0,1]\), and let \(\iota\) be a finite
candidate index type.  Suppose a bad event \(B(\omega)\) is covered by a
family of candidate events \(C_i(\omega)\), in the sense that every
\(\omega\) satisfying \(B\) satisfies \(C_i\) for at least one \(i\in\iota\).
If each candidate event has
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m,p,C_i)\le q
\]
then
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m,p,B)
\le |\iota|\,q .
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
First compare \(B\) with the union of the candidate events.  By the covering
hypothesis,
\[
B(\omega)\Rightarrow \exists i\in\iota,\ C_i(\omega).
\]
Expanding \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\) as the finite sum of
\(\noderef{TwoBiteSampleWeight}\) over the satisfying samples, this event
inclusion implies
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m,p,B)
\le
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}
  (n,m,p,\omega\mapsto \exists i\in\iota,\ C_i(\omega)).
\]
The only point needed for this monotonicity is nonnegativity of the sample
weights, which follows from \(\noderef{TwoBiteSampleWeightNonnegative}\)
because \(0\le p\le1\).

Apply \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbabilityUnionBound}\) to the finite family
\((C_i)_{i\in\iota}\).  This gives
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}
  (n,m,p,\omega\mapsto \exists i\in\iota,\ C_i(\omega))
\le
\sum_{i\in\iota}
  \noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m,p,C_i).
\]
By the assumed fixed-candidate bound, every summand is at most \(q\).  Summing
these inequalities over the finite index type yields
\[
\sum_{i\in\iota}
  \noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m,p,C_i)
\le
\sum_{i\in\iota} q
=|\iota|\,q.
\]
Combining the three displayed inequalities proves the claimed finite
candidate union bound.
\end{proof}
