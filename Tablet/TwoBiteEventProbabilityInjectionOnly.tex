\begin{lemma}
For an event \(\mathcal E\) depending only on the injection coordinate \(\pi\in V(G)\hookrightarrow V_R\times V_B\), the two-bite event probability satisfies
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m,p)(\mathcal E) = \#\{\pi:\mathcal E(\pi)\} \cdot \noderef{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m).
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{TwoBiteSampleWeight}\), the sample weight is \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_R)\,\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G_B)\,\noderef{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)\).
Summing \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G)\) over all graphs \(G\) gives \(1\) by \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeightSumOne}\).
Since the event depends only on \(\pi\), the sum factors, leaving the count of satisfying injections multiplied by \(\noderef{UniformInjectionWeight}(n,m)\).
\end{proof}
