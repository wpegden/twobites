\begin{helper}
For every \(m\in\mathbb N\) and \(p\in\mathbb R\), the
\(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}\) weights on all simple graphs on \(\mathrm{Fin}(m)\)
have total mass \(1\):
\[
\sum_G \noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G)=1.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Identify a simple graph on \(\mathrm{Fin}(m)\) with the Boolean choices on the
finite set of ordered pairs \((i,j)\) with \(i<j\).  Under this identification,
\(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G)\) is the product over these pairs of \(p\) when
the corresponding edge is present and \(1-p\) when it is absent.  Summing over
all graphs is therefore the product, over all possible edge positions, of the
two-term sum \(p+(1-p)\).  Each factor is \(1\), so the total product is \(1\).
\end{proof}
