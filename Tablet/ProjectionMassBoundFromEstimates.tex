\begin{helper}
Let \(0\le\varepsilon_2\le1\) and put \(\delta=\varepsilon_2/100\).  Suppose
that nonnegative mass \(T\), source size \(S\), and deficit \(D\) satisfy
\[
(1-\delta)T\le S
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
(1-2\delta)S\le D.
\]
Then
\[
T\le (1+\varepsilon_2)D.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Multiplying the first inequality by the nonnegative factor \(1-2\delta\) and
then using the second gives
\[
(1-\delta)(1-2\delta)T\le D.
\]
For \(0\le\varepsilon_2\le1\) and \(\delta=\varepsilon_2/100\), the elementary
polynomial inequality
\[
1\le (1+\varepsilon_2)(1-\delta)(1-2\delta)
\]
holds.  Since \(T\ge0\), this implies
\[
T\le (1+\varepsilon_2)(1-\delta)(1-2\delta)T
\le (1+\varepsilon_2)D,
\]
as required.
\end{proof}
