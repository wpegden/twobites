\begin{helper}
For the T4 notation
\[
L=\log n,\quad k_R=(1+\eta)\sqrt{nL},\quad
K=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalIndependenceScale}(1+\eta,n),\quad
k=(K:\mathbb R),
\]
and \(m_R=n/L^2\), \(m=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n)\),
assume \(n>0\), \(L>0\), \(m>0\), \(k_R\ge0\), \(2\le k\),
\(k_R\le k\), \(m\le m_R\), and
\[
(2kL+L)\bigl(16Lm_R n^{8\eta}\bigr)\le k_R^4 .
\]
Then
\[
2kL+L\le
\frac{\noderef{RealChooseTwo}(k)^2}{Lm n^{8\eta}}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \(\noderef{RealChooseTwoQuadraticBounds}\), since \(2\le k\),
\[
\frac{k^2}{4}\le \noderef{RealChooseTwo}(k).
\]
In particular \(\noderef{RealChooseTwo}(k)\ge0\), and squaring gives
\[
k^4\le16\,\noderef{RealChooseTwo}(k)^2.
\]
Because \(0\le k_R\le k\), we also have \(k_R^4\le k^4\).
The hypotheses \(L>0\), \(m>0\), \(n>0\), and \(m\le m_R\) imply
\[
16Lm n^{8\eta}\le16Lm_R n^{8\eta}.
\]
Moreover \(2kL+L\ge0\).  Therefore the assumed threshold inequality yields
\[
(2kL+L)(16Lm n^{8\eta})
\le16\,\noderef{RealChooseTwo}(k)^2.
\]
Dividing by \(16\) and then by the positive quantity \(Lm n^{8\eta}\)
proves the asserted lower bound on the exponent magnitude.
\end{proof}
