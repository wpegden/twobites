\begin{lemma}
Fix red and blue center sets \(B_R,B_B\), red and blue projection sets
\(P_R,P_B\), and an integer \(K\).  Suppose
\[
|P_R|\le K,\qquad |P_B|\le K.
\]
Let \(p\ge0\), \(A>0\), and suppose
\[
p\,(|B_R|+|B_B|)K\le \frac{A}{4e}.
\]
If a fixed-pair bad event has probability, or product weight, at most the
\(t\)-subset union-bound quantity with
\[
N=|B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|,\qquad t=\lceil A\rceil,
\]
namely
\[
\binom Nt p^t,
\]
then the bad event has probability, or product weight, at most
\[
\exp(-A/4).
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The candidate red set has at most \(|B_R||P_R|\) entries, and the candidate
blue set has at most \(|B_B||P_B|\) entries.  Hence
\[
N=|B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|
\le |B_R|K+|B_B|K=(|B_R|+|B_B|)K.
\]
Multiplying by \(p\ge0\), the assumed mean bound gives
\[
pN\le p(|B_R|+|B_B|)K\le \frac{A}{4e}.
\]
The fixed-candidate binomial tail estimate
\(\noderef{MediumClosedPairsFixedCandidateBinomialTail}\), applied with this
candidate count \(N\), now gives the bound \(\exp(-A/4)\).
\end{proof}
