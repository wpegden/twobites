\begin{helper}
Let \(A\) be a finite set and let \(\pi:A\to B\) be a projection map.  Suppose
that every fiber of \(\pi\) inside \(A\) has size at most \(F\).  Then
\[
|A|\le F\,|\pi(A)|.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Partition \(A\) according to the value of \(\pi\).  The projection values are
the elements of \(\pi(A)\), and the part over \(y\in\pi(A)\) is
\[
\{a\in A:\pi(a)=y\}.
\]
The map \(a\mapsto(\pi(a),a)\) injects \(A\) into the disjoint union of these
fibers, so
\[
|A|\le\sum_{y\in\pi(A)}|\{a\in A:\pi(a)=y\}|.
\]
Each summand is at most \(F\), hence the sum is at most
\[
\sum_{y\in\pi(A)}F=F|\pi(A)|.
\]
\end{proof}
