\begin{helper}
If a two-bite event has strictly positive
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\), then some sample in that event has
strictly positive \(\noderef{TwoBiteSampleWeight}\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By definition, \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\) is the finite sum of
\(\noderef{TwoBiteSampleWeight}(\omega)\) over the samples \(\omega\) that
belong to the event.  If every sample in the event had nonpositive weight, then
the finite sum would be nonpositive.  This contradicts strict positivity of the
event probability.  Hence at least one sample in the event has strictly
positive weight.
\end{proof}
