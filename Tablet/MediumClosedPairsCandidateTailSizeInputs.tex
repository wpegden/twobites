\begin{helper}
Assume the parameter hierarchy.  Eventually in \(n\), let
\[
  K=K(n) \quad\text{and}\quad
  S=\left\lceil
    \frac{K n^{1/4-\epsilon}+L_{\mathrm{large}}(\epsilon,n)}
         {L_{\mathrm{small}}(\epsilon,n)}
  \right\rceil .
\]
For every ambient size \(m\), every deterministic candidate index consisting
of a \(K\)-set \(I\subseteq[n]\) and an \(S\)-tuple of base vertices, and every
embedding cell \(\pi\), let \(\operatorname{coverB}\) be the image of the
\(S\)-tuple.  Let \(B_R\) and \(B_B\) be the red and blue centers in
\(\operatorname{coverB}\), and let \(P_R\) and \(P_B\) be the two coordinate
projections of \(\pi(I)\).  Then
\[
  |B_R|+|B_B|
    \le \frac32 K n^{1/4-3\epsilon},
  \qquad |P_R|\le K,\qquad |P_B|\le K .
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
By \noderef{ParameterHierarchyBaseThreshold}, eventually
\[
  K\ge (1+\epsilon)\sqrt{n\log n}.
\]
Taking \(n\) still larger if necessary, we also have \(\log n\ge 1\) and
\(8\le n^{1/4-2\epsilon}\); this is valid since \(\epsilon<1/16\).  These
inequalities imply
\[
  n^{1/4-\epsilon}+1
    \le \frac12 K n^{1/4-3\epsilon}.
\]
Indeed \(n^{1/4-\epsilon}=n^{1/4-3\epsilon}n^{2\epsilon}\), while
\(8n^{2\epsilon}\le K\); similarly \(8\le K n^{1/4-3\epsilon}\).

\noderef{MediumClosedPairsWitnessSizeCeilPackage} gives
\[
  S\le K n^{1/4-3\epsilon}+n^{1/4-\epsilon}+1,
\]
so the preceding absorption gives
\[
  S\le \frac32 K n^{1/4-3\epsilon}.
\]
The cover \(\operatorname{coverB}\) is the image of a function from
\(\operatorname{Fin}(S)\), hence \(|\operatorname{coverB}|\le S\).
The red and blue center images are disjoint subsets of
\(\operatorname{coverB}\), and their cardinalities are exactly
\(|B_R|\) and \(|B_B|\), so
\[
  |B_R|+|B_B|\le |\operatorname{coverB}|\le S.
\]
Combining this with the bound on \(S\) proves the required center-size
estimate.  Finally, \(P_R\) and \(P_B\) are images of the same \(K\)-element
set \(I\), so each has cardinality at most \(K\).
\end{proof}
