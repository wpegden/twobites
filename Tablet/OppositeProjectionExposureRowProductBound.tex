\begin{helper}
Let \(U_0,V_0,T\subseteq\operatorname{Fin}n\), let
\(\rho:\operatorname{Fin}n\to\operatorname{Fin}m\), and let
\(\eta:U_0\to\operatorname{Fin}m\).  Put \(S=\eta(U_0)\).  Let
\(X_{\mathrm{all},\eta}\) be the finite set of injections
\(\phi:\operatorname{Fin}n\hookrightarrow
\operatorname{Fin}m\times\operatorname{Fin}m\) such that
\((\phi(v))_1=\rho(v)\) for every \(v\) and
\((\phi(u))_2=\eta(u)\) for every \(u\in U_0\).  Let
\(X_{\mathrm{good},\eta}(T)\subseteq X_{\mathrm{all},\eta}\) be the subset
where \((\phi(v))_2\in S\) for every \(v\in T\).  Assume \(T\subseteq V_0\),
assume
\[
u\in U_0,\ v\in V_0\quad\Longrightarrow\quad \rho(u)\ne\rho(v),
\]
and assume \(X_{\mathrm{all},\eta}\) is nonempty.  For each row
\(i\in\operatorname{Fin}m\), put
\[
R_i=\{v:\rho(v)=i\}.
\]
Then
\[
\frac{|X_{\mathrm{good},\eta}(T)|}{|X_{\mathrm{all},\eta}|}
\le
\prod_i
\left(\frac{|S|}{m}\right)^{|T\cap R_i|}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Factor the eta-conditioned denominator and numerator over red rows.  In row
\(i\), the denominator row count \(d_i\) counts injective blue-coordinate
assignments on \(R_i\) agreeing with \(\eta\) on \(U_0\cap R_i\), and the
numerator row count \(g_i\) additionally requires the vertices of
\(T\cap R_i\) to land in \(S\).  The nonemptiness of
\(X_{\mathrm{all},\eta}\) implies \(\prod_i d_i\ne0\), hence every
\(d_i\ne0\).

If \(T\cap R_i=\varnothing\), then \(g_i=d_i\), so the row ratio is \(1\).
If \(T\cap R_i\ne\varnothing\), choose \(v\in T\cap R_i\).  Since
\(T\subseteq V_0\), this vertex lies in \(V_0\).  The row-disjointness
hypothesis then implies \(U_0\cap R_i=\varnothing\), so the \(\eta\)-condition
is absent in this row.  The row ratio is therefore the ordinary row-injection
ratio bounded by \(\noderef{OppositeProjectionRowInjectionCounting}\), namely
\[
\frac{g_i}{d_i}\le
\left(\frac{|S|}{m}\right)^{|T\cap R_i|}.
\]
Multiplying these row inequalities over \(i\) gives the stated product
bound.
\end{proof}
