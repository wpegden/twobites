\begin{helper}
Let \(L=\log n\).  If \(n>0\), \(L\ge0\), and
\[
\frac{200L^3}{n^\eta}\le1,
\]
then
\[
100(n^{1/4}+1/2)L^3\le n^{1/4+\eta}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Since \(n\) is a positive integer, \(n\ge1\), so \(n^{1/4}\ge1\).  Hence
\[
n^{1/4}+1/2\le2n^{1/4}.
\]
Therefore
\[
100(n^{1/4}+1/2)L^3\le200n^{1/4}L^3.
\]
The threshold hypothesis gives \(200L^3\le n^\eta\).  Multiplying this
inequality by the nonnegative number \(n^{1/4}\) yields
\[
200n^{1/4}L^3\le n^{1/4}n^\eta=n^{1/4+\eta}.
\]
\end{proof}
