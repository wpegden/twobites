\begin{helper}
Let \(u,v,w\) be three lifted vertices whose three pairs are red overlay edges.
For the red projected-pair rank used in \(\noderef{TwoBiteFinalGraph}\), one of
the three red projected pairs \(uv\), \(uw\), or \(vw\) is strictly latest:
either the ranks of \(uw\) and \(vw\) are both smaller than the rank of \(uv\),
or the ranks of \(uv\) and \(vw\) are both smaller than the rank of \(uw\), or
the ranks of \(uv\) and \(uw\) are both smaller than the rank of \(vw\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Write \(r_x\) for the red projection of a lifted vertex \(x\).  Since
\(uv,uw,vw\) are red overlay edges, the definition of
\(\noderef{TwoBiteOverlay}\) gives red base adjacencies
\(r_ur_v\), \(r_ur_w\), and \(r_vr_w\).  The red base graph is a simple graph,
so it is loopless.  Therefore \(r_u\ne r_v\), \(r_u\ne r_w\), and
\(r_v\ne r_w\).

Applying \(\noderef{FinPairRankLatestOfDistinct}\) to the three pairwise
distinct red projections \(r_u,r_v,r_w\), one of the three projected red pairs
has rank strictly larger than the other two.  If the largest rank is that of
\(uv\), we obtain the first alternative in the statement.  If it is that of
\(uw\), we obtain the second alternative.  If it is that of \(vw\), we obtain
the third alternative.
\end{proof}
