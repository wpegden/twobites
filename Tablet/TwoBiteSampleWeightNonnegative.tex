\begin{helper}
For every two-bite sample \(\omega\), if \(0\le p\le 1\), then the sample
weight \(\noderef{TwoBiteSampleWeight}(\omega)\) is nonnegative.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The sample weight is the product of the red graph weight, the blue graph
weight, and the uniform injection weight.  Each graph weight is a finite
product whose factors are either \(p\) or \(1-p\), both nonnegative when
\(0\le p\le1\).  The uniform injection factor is either \(0\) or the inverse
of a positive natural cardinal cast to \(\mathbb R\), hence is nonnegative.
The product of these three nonnegative factors is nonnegative.
\end{proof}
