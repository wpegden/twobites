\begin{helper}
Let \(m(A)\) be a nonnegative mass on complete terminal assignments
\(A\subseteq U\) with total mass at most \(1\).  Suppose a finite family of
released residual blocks is indexed by realized branch/transcript leaves,
each block is contained in \(2^U\), each released mass is the sum of \(m\)
over its block, and blocks for distinct realized leaves are disjoint.  Then
the sum of the released masses over all realized leaves is at most \(1\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
SKETCH:
Sum the exact block-mass identities over the finite realized leaf set.
Pairwise disjointness turns the double sum over blocks into the mass of their
union.  Since every block assignment lies in \(2^U\) and all masses are
nonnegative, the mass of the union is bounded by the total mass over
\(2^U\), which is at most \(1\).
\end{proof}
