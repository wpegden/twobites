\begin{helper}
Fix \(B,J\) in the blue-branch interface.  Suppose the realized-cell
geometry, complete-assignment compatibility, residual scan stability, and
first-mismatch uniqueness hypotheses are available.  Then for every realized
branch/transcript pair \((\beta,\tau)\) there is a released residual block
\(\mathcal R_{\beta,\tau}\subseteq 2^U\).  Membership in the block is
equivalent to compatibility of the reconstructed complete terminal
assignment, the full released cylinder for \((\beta,\tau)\) is contained in
the block, and blocks for distinct realized pairs are disjoint.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
SKETCH:
Define \(\mathcal R_{\beta,\tau}\) to be the set of residual assignments
whose reconstruction after restoring the fixed supports and selected
absences is accepted by the deterministic scan as compatible with
\((\beta,\tau)\).  The forward and converse compatibility hypotheses give the
membership equivalence.  Residual scan stability puts every assignment in the
displayed released cylinder into the block.  First-mismatch uniqueness says
that one residual assignment cannot reconstruct compatibly for two different
realized branch/transcript pairs, which proves disjointness.
\end{proof}
