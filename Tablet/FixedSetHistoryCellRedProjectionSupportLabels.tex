\begin{helper}
Let \(I\subseteq \operatorname{Fin}(n)\), let \(H\) be a history cell with a
fixed embedding representative \(\omega_0\), and let \(T\subseteq H\) be a
target event.  Assume every sample in \(H\) satisfies the projection-size
event with parameters \((k,\ell_R,\ell_B)\), every staged-open coordinate in
\(H\) lies in the terminal set \(U\), and every target sample has red present
staged-open support of size \(u_R\).  Then there is a natural number
\(N_R\le k^2\) and a finite family of red terminal support labels of size at
most \(\binom{N_R}{u_R}\) which contains the red present staged-open support
of every target sample.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
SKETCH:
The history cell fixes the embedding, so the red projection of \(I\) is the
same for every sample in the cell as for the representative \(\omega_0\).
Let \(U_R\) be the red terminal coordinate universe generated by pairs in
this projected set.  Its cardinality is at most \(k^2\).  The label family is
the collection of all \(u_R\)-element subsets of \(U_R\) that are contained
in the terminal set \(U\).  Every member is red, terminal, and has size
\(u_R\), and there are at most \(\binom{|U_R|}{u_R}\) such labels.  The
fixed-embedding and staged-open hypotheses put every target red support
inside \(U_R\cap U\), and the fixed-count hypothesis gives its cardinality
\(u_R\).
\end{proof}
