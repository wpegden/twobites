\begin{helper}
Let \(n,m\) be natural numbers, let \(U_0,T\subseteq \operatorname{Fin} n\),
let \(\rho:\operatorname{Fin}n\to\operatorname{Fin}m\), and let
\(\eta:U_0\to\operatorname{Fin}m\).  Put
\[
S=\operatorname{im}(\eta),\qquad R_i=\{v:\rho(v)=i\}.
\]
Let \(X_{\mathrm{all},\eta}\) be the set of injections with first coordinate
\(\rho\) and eta-conditioned second coordinate on \(U_0\), and let
\(X_{\mathrm{good},\eta}(T)\) be the subset for which every \(v\in T\) has
second coordinate in \(S\).  For each row \(i\), let \(g_i\) be the number
of injective maps \(f:R_i\to\operatorname{Fin}m\) satisfying the eta
condition on \(U_0\cap R_i\) and also sending every \(v\in T\cap R_i\) into
\(S\).  Then
\[
|X_{\mathrm{good},\eta}(T)|=\prod_i g_i.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Use the same row decomposition as for the all-set product.  A good global
injection \(\phi\) determines row maps \(f_i(v)=(\phi(v))_2\).  The
argument using equality of first coordinates shows that each \(f_i\) is
injective on \(R_i\), and the eta condition on \(\phi\) gives the row eta
condition on \(U_0\cap R_i\).  In addition, if \(v\in T\cap R_i\), then the
goodness of \(\phi\) gives \(f_i(v)=(\phi(v))_2\in S\).  Hence the row map
in row \(i\) is counted by \(g_i\).

Conversely, given a tuple of row maps counted by the \(g_i\), define
\(\phi(v)=(\rho(v),f_{\rho(v)}(v))\).  Rowwise injectivity again gives
global injectivity because different rows have different first coordinates.
The row eta conditions put \(\phi\) in \(X_{\mathrm{all},\eta}\), and the
row good conditions say exactly that every vertex of \(T\cap R_i\) maps
into \(S\) in row \(i\).  Since the rows partition \(\operatorname{Fin} n\),
these row conditions are equivalent to the global condition defining
\(X_{\mathrm{good},\eta}(T)\).  The two constructions are inverse, so
\(X_{\mathrm{good},\eta}(T)\) is in bijection with the finite cartesian
product of the row sets counted by \(g_i\).  Taking cardinalities gives the
product identity.
\end{proof}
