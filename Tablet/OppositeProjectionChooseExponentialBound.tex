\begin{lemma}
For all natural numbers \(N\) and \(t\),
\[
  \binom Nt \le \left(\frac{eN}{t}\right)^t,
\]
with the usual convention that the case \(t=0\) gives \(1\le 1\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The case \(t=0\) is immediate.  For \(t>0\), the standard binomial estimate gives
\[
  \binom Nt \le \frac{N^t}{t!}.
\]
Stirling's lower bound gives
\[
  \left(\frac{t}{e}\right)^t\le t!.
\]
Since \(N^t\ge 0\), dividing by the larger denominator \(t!\) only decreases the quotient, so
\[
  \frac{N^t}{t!}\le
  \frac{N^t}{(t/e)^t}
  =
  \left(\frac{eN}{t}\right)^t.
\]
Combining the two inequalities proves the claim.
\end{proof}
