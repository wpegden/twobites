\begin{helper}
Fix \(I\subseteq\mathrm{Fin}(n)\) with \(|I|=k\).  For every event \(A\),
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(A)
=\sum_{\ell_R=0}^k\sum_{\ell_B=0}^k
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\bigl(A\cap
\noderef{TwoBiteProjectionSizeEvent}(I,k,\ell_R,\ell_B)\bigr).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
For any sample \(\omega\), the red image size
\(|I.\mathrm{image}(\pi_R^\omega)|\) and blue image size
\(|I.\mathrm{image}(\pi_B^\omega)|\) are uniquely determined.  Each is at
most \(|I|=k\), so the pair lies in \(\{0,\ldots,k\}^2\).  With this pair,
\(\omega\) satisfies the corresponding
\(\noderef{TwoBiteProjectionSizeEvent}\).  Conversely, every sample in one of
these cell intersections is in \(A\).  Thus the cells cover \(A\).

The cells are disjoint: if a sample lies in two cells, the two red cardinality
equalities force the red indices to be equal, and the two blue cardinality
equalities force the blue indices to be equal.  Since
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\) is the finite sum of sample weights,
finite additivity over this disjoint finite partition gives the displayed
double-sum identity.
\end{proof}
