\begin{helper}
Let \(\eta,\varepsilon_1\in\mathbb R\) and \(n\in\mathbb N\).  Put
\[
L=\log n,\quad
K=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalIndependenceScale}(1+\eta,n),\quad k=(K:\mathbb R),
\]
\[
M=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n),\quad m=(M:\mathbb R),
\quad
p=\frac12\sqrt{\frac Ln},\quad
t_1=\frac{\sqrt{nL}}{\log L}.
\]
Assume \(0<\varepsilon_1\), \(0<n\), \(0<L\), \(0<\log L\), \(0\le k\),
\[
2pm\le\frac{\sqrt n}{L^{3/2}},
\]
and
\[
\frac{2\log L}{L^2}\le\frac{\varepsilon_1}{10}.
\]
Then
\[
\frac{2k}{t_1}(2pm)\le\frac{\varepsilon_1}{10}k.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The quantity \(t_1=\sqrt{nL}/\log L\) is positive, so
\(\frac{2k}{t_1}\ge0\).  Multiplying the mass bound by this nonnegative
factor gives
\[
\frac{2k}{t_1}(2pm)
\le \frac{2k}{t_1}\frac{\sqrt n}{L^{3/2}}.
\]
Using \(t_1=\sqrt{nL}/\log L\), \(\sqrt{nL}=\sqrt n\,\sqrt L\), and
\(L^{3/2}=L\sqrt L\), the right side is exactly
\[
k\frac{2\log L}{L^2}.
\]
The threshold bound and \(k\ge0\) therefore imply
\[
k\frac{2\log L}{L^2}\le k\frac{\varepsilon_1}{10}
=\frac{\varepsilon_1}{10}k.
\]
Combining the inequalities proves the claim.
\end{proof}
