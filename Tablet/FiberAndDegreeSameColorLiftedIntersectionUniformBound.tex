\begin{helper}[Uniform fixed-graphs same-color lifted intersection bound]
Let \(n,m\in\mathbb N\) and \(p\in\mathbb R\) satisfy
\[
m=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n),\qquad
p=\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}(1/2,n),
\]
\[
n\le m^2,\qquad 1\le \log n,\qquad
n\le 2(\log n)^2m.
\]
Fix red and blue base graphs \(G_R,G_B\) on \(\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) with
decidable adjacency.  Assume the same-color base-codegree bounds
\[
\forall r\ne s,\quad
\bigl|\{t\in\operatorname{Fin}(m):G_R.Adj(r,t)\land G_R.Adj(s,t)\}\bigr|
\le \log n
\]
and
\[
\forall b\ne c,\quad
\bigl|\{t\in\operatorname{Fin}(m):G_B.Adj(b,t)\land G_B.Adj(c,t)\}\bigr|
\le \log n.
\]
Let \(C(\omega)\) be the fixed-base-graphs condition
\(\omega.1=G_R\land\omega.2.1=G_B\).  Let \(E(\omega)\) be the event that
either there are \(r\ne s\in\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) with
\[
\bigl|
\noderef{TwoBiteLiftedNeighborhood}(\omega,\operatorname{inl}(r))
\cap
\noderef{TwoBiteLiftedNeighborhood}(\omega,\operatorname{inl}(s))
\bigr|>100(\log n)^3
\]
or there are \(b\ne c\in\operatorname{Fin}(m)\) with
\[
\bigl|
\noderef{TwoBiteLiftedNeighborhood}(\omega,\operatorname{inr}(b))
\cap
\noderef{TwoBiteLiftedNeighborhood}(\omega,\operatorname{inr}(c))
\bigr|>100(\log n)^3.
\]
Then
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteConditionalProbability}(n,m,p;E\mid C)
\le
2m^2\exp(-2(\log n)^3).
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
In a fixed base-graph cell, the only random coordinate is the injection
\(\operatorname{Fin}(n)\hookrightarrow \operatorname{Fin}(m)\times
\operatorname{Fin}(m)\), and
\(\noderef{TwoBiteFixedGraphsConditionalInjectionBridge}\) expands the
conditional probability as a uniform count of injections.  For each ordered
red pair,
\(\noderef{FiberAndDegreeSameColorLiftedIntersectionFixedPairInjectionTail}\)
bounds the count of injections whose image hits the first-coordinate common
neighbor set too often.  For each ordered blue pair,
\(\noderef{FiberAndDegreeSameColorLiftedIntersectionSecondCoordinateTail}\)
gives the corresponding second-coordinate bound.  A finite union bound over
the fewer than \(m^2\) red ordered pairs and fewer than \(m^2\) blue ordered
pairs gives the displayed \(2m^2\exp(-2(\log n)^3)\) bound.
\end{proof}
