\begin{helper}
For every \(n\) and every finite set \(X\subseteq \mathrm{Fin}(n)\),
\[
\left|\noderef{TwoBiteFinalPairs}(X)\right|
\le \frac{|X|^2}{2}.
\]
Here \(\noderef{TwoBiteFinalPairs}(X)\) is the rank-oriented set of final
vertex pairs from \(X\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Let
\[
A=\noderef{TwoBiteFinalPairs}(X)
\]
and let
\[
\tau(A)=\{(v,u):(u,v)\in A\}
\]
be the swapped copy of \(A\).  By the definition of
\(\noderef{TwoBiteFinalPairs}\), an element \((u,v)\in A\) has
\(u,v\in X\) and \(u.val<v.val\).

The sets \(A\) and \(\tau(A)\) are disjoint.  If
\((x,y)\in A\cap\tau(A)\), then \((x,y)=(v,u)\) for some \((u,v)\in A\).
Membership of \((x,y)\) in \(A\) gives \(x.val<y.val\).  Membership of
\((u,v)\) in \(A\) gives \(u.val<v.val\), which after
\((x,y)=(v,u)\) says \(y.val<x.val\).  This is impossible.

Both \(A\) and \(\tau(A)\) lie in \(X\times X\): this is direct for \(A\),
and for \(\tau(A)\) it follows by swapping the two endpoints of a pair whose
endpoints already lie in \(X\).  Hence
\[
A\cup\tau(A)\subseteq X\times X.
\]
The swap map is injective, so \(|\tau(A)|=|A|\).  Since \(A\) and
\(\tau(A)\) are disjoint,
\[
|A\cup\tau(A)|=|A|+|\tau(A)|=2|A|.
\]
Taking cardinalities in the subset \(A\cup\tau(A)\subseteq X\times X\) gives
\[
2|A|\le |X\times X|=|X|^2.
\]
Dividing by \(2\) over \(\mathbb R\) yields
\[
|A|\le\frac{|X|^2}{2},
\]
which is the claimed estimate for \(\noderef{TwoBiteFinalPairs}(X)\).
\end{proof}
