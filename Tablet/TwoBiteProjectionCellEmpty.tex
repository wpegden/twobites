\begin{helper}
Let \(I\subseteq\mathrm{Fin}(n)\).  A projection-size cell
\(\noderef{TwoBiteProjectionSizeEvent}(I,k,\ell_R,\ell_B,\omega)\) is empty
whenever \(\ell_R\ell_B<k\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Inside the cell, \(|I|=k\), the red projection image of \(I\) has size
\(\ell_R\), and the blue projection image has size \(\ell_B\).  The embedded
image of \(I\) under \(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}\) has cardinality \(k\),
because the embedding is injective.  This embedded image lies inside the
Cartesian product of the red and blue projection images, whose cardinality is
\(\ell_R\ell_B\).  Therefore \(k\le \ell_R\ell_B\), contradicting
\(\ell_R\ell_B<k\).
\end{proof}
