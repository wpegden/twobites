\begin{lemma}
For every real constant \(A>0\), eventually in \(n\),
\[
A(\log n)^3
  \leq
  \operatorname{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}(1/2,n)\,
  \operatorname{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n).
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Let \(L=\log n\), \(M=\operatorname{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n)\), and
\(p=\operatorname{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}(1/2,n)\).  The definitions of
\(p\) and \(M\) are those of \noderef{TwoBiteEdgeProbability} and
\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}.  By
\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexRatioEventually}, eventually
\[
n \leq 2L^2M .
\]
Also \((\log n)^9=o(n)\), so for the fixed constant \(A\) we eventually have
\[
16A^2L^9 \leq n .
\]
For such \(n\), with \(L>0\), squaring the first displayed inequality gives
\[
n^2\leq 4L^4M^2 .
\]
Multiplying the second displayed inequality by \(n\) and combining these two
inequalities yields
\[
16A^2L^9n \leq 4L^4M^2.
\]
After division by \(4L^3>0\), this is
\[
4A^2L^6 n \leq L M^2 .
\]
Since \(p=(1/2)\sqrt{L/n}\), the last inequality is exactly
\[
(A L^3)^2 \leq (pM)^2 .
\]
Both sides being squared are nonnegative, hence \(A L^3\leq pM\), as required.
\end{proof}
