\begin{helper}[Projection size analytic error absorption]
\label{helper:ProjectionSizeAnalyticErrorAbsorption}
For any $\delta > 0$ and $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists an integer $n_0$ such that for all $n \ge n_0$, if we let $L = \log n$, then $L \ge 1$ and
$$ 3 + 2 \left( \frac{5}{2} \log L + \log(4(1+\varepsilon)) \right) \le \delta L. $$
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
This follows since $\log L = o(L)$ as $L \to \infty$, and $L \to \infty$ as $n \to \infty$. Thus, the linear term $\delta L$ eventually dominates the constant and logarithmic terms.
\end{proof}
