\begin{helper}[FiberAndDegreeMixedLiftedIntersectionNegligible]
\label{helper:FiberAndDegreeMixedLiftedIntersectionNegligible}
Let \(\beta=1/2\), let
\[
m(n)=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n),
\qquad
p(n)=\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}(\beta,n),
\]
and let probabilities be taken with respect to
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,m(n),p(n))\).  The following event has
negligible probability in the sense of \(\noderef{NegligibleProbability}\):
there exist \(r,b\in\operatorname{Fin}(m(n))\) such that
\[
\left|
\noderef{TwoBiteLiftedNeighborhood}(\omega,\operatorname{inl}(r))
\cap
\noderef{TwoBiteLiftedNeighborhood}(\omega,\operatorname{inr}(b))
\right|
>100(\log n)^3,
\]
and, simultaneously, all red and blue base degrees are bounded by
\[
2p(n)m(n):
\quad
\forall r\in\operatorname{Fin}(m(n)),\ 
\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(\omega.1,r)\le 2p(n)m(n),
\]
\[
\forall b\in\operatorname{Fin}(m(n)),\ 
\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(\omega.2.1,b)\le 2p(n)m(n).
\]
\end{helper}

\begin{proof}
Write \(M=m(n)\), \(p=p(n)\), and \(L=\log n\).  Let \(E_n\) be exactly the
event in the statement: a bad mixed lifted intersection occurs and all red
and blue base degrees are at most \(2pM\).
We may discard finitely many \(n\).  By
\(\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalTotalMassOneEventually}\), eventually
\[
n\le M^2,
\]
and because \(L=\log n\to\infty\), eventually \(L\ge1\).  Also the hypotheses
\(\beta=1/2\) and \(m(n)=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n)\)
identify \(p\) with \(\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeProbability}(1/2,n)\).

Fix base graphs \(G_R,G_B\) on \(\operatorname{Fin}(M)\).  If either
\[
\exists r,\quad \noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G_R,r)>2pM
\]
or
\[
\exists b,\quad \noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G_B,b)>2pM,
\]
then no two-bite sample whose base graphs are \(G_R,G_B\) can lie in
\(E_n\), because the degree-good conjunct in \(E_n\) is false.  Hence the
conditional probability of \(E_n\) given these base graphs is \(0\).

If instead the fixed base graphs satisfy the degree bounds, then the event
\(E_n\) is contained in the event that some mixed lifted intersection is
larger than \(100L^3\).  The hypothesis required by
\(\noderef{FiberAndDegreeMixedLiftedIntersectionUniformBound}\) consists of
the specialized parameter identities and eventual inequalities just recorded,
together with the fixed-graph degree bound
\[
\forall r,b,\quad
\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G_R,r)\le2pM
\quad\text{and}\quad
\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G_B,b)\le2pM.
\]
Therefore, for every fixed pair of base graphs,
\[
\Pr(E_n\mid G_R,G_B)\le M^2\exp(-2L^3).
\tag{1}
\]

Apply \(\noderef{FiberAndDegreeMixedLiftedIntersectionConditioning}\) to the
event \(E_n\) with \(C=M^2\exp(-2L^3)\).  The bound (1) is uniform over all
fixed base graphs, so the unconditional probability satisfies
\[
\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}(n,M,p)(E_n)
\le M^2\exp(-2L^3).
\tag{2}
\]
Finally, \(\noderef{OppositeProjectionAsymptoticBound}\), applied with
\(c=2\) and \(m(n)=\noderef{TwoBiteNaturalReducedVertexCount}(n)\), gives
\[
M(n)^2\exp(-2(\log n)^3)\to0.
\]
Combining this convergence with (2) proves that the sequence of probabilities
in the statement tends to \(0\), which is precisely the
\(\noderef{NegligibleProbability}\) conclusion.
\end{proof}
