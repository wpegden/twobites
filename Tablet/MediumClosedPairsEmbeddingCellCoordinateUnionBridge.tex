\begin{lemma}
Fix an embedding \(\pi:\mathrm{Fin}(n)\hookrightarrow
\mathrm{Fin}(m)\times\mathrm{Fin}(m)\), finite red centers \(B_R\), blue
centers \(B_B\), red projections \(P_R\), and blue projections \(P_B\).
Assume \(0<p<1\).  Let \(C\) be the finite set of tagged normalized nonloop
candidate coordinates obtained as follows: for each nonloop ordered pair in
\(B_R\times P_R\), normalize the two endpoints to increasing order and tag the
coordinate red; do the analogous construction for \(B_B\times P_B\) and tag
the coordinate blue.

Then the \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\) of the event that
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}(\omega)=\pi\) and at least \(t\) coordinates of
\(C\) are present, where presence is evaluated by
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEdgeCoordinateValue}\), is at most
\[
\left(\binom{|B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|}{t}p^t\right)
\Pr(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}(\omega)=\pi).
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Let \(C_R\) and \(C_B\) be the red and blue normalized coordinate sets, and
let \(C=C_R\cup C_B\).  Since \(C_R\) is an image of the nonloop subset of
\(B_R\times P_R\), its cardinality is at most \(|B_R||P_R|\).  Similarly
\(|C_B|\le |B_B||P_B|\).  Hence
\[
|C|\le |B_R||P_R|+|B_B||P_B|.
\]

For any \(t\)-element subset \(A\subseteq C\), every coordinate in \(A\) is
normalized and nonloop, hence its endpoints are in increasing order.  Apply
\(\noderef{FixedSetCylinderProductMassFactor}\) to the cylinder
\(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}(\omega)=\pi\), with no recorded graph
coordinates and with product set \(A\).  The representative sample is any
sample with embedding \(\pi\).  The cylinder condition is exactly the statement
that the embedding coordinate is fixed to \(\pi\), and there are no recorded
coordinates.  Taking the selected subset in the product law to be all of
\(A\), the product event is precisely that every coordinate of \(A\) is
present.  Thus
\[
\Pr(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}(\omega)=\pi
  \text{ and all coordinates in }A\text{ are present})
=p^{|A|}
  \Pr(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}(\omega)=\pi)
=p^t\Pr(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}(\omega)=\pi).
\]

If a sample in the embedding cell has at least \(t\) present coordinates of
\(C\), finite subset selection gives a \(t\)-element subset
\(A\subseteq C\) all of whose coordinates are present.  Therefore this event
is contained in the union over \(A\in\binom Ct\) of the fixed-\(A\) events
above.  By \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbabilityUnionBound}\), using
nonnegativity of the two-bite sample weights, its probability is at most
\[
\binom{|C|}{t}p^t
\Pr(\noderef{TwoBiteEmbedding}(\omega)=\pi).
\]
The cardinality bound on \(C\), monotonicity of binomial coefficients, and
nonnegativity of the final factor give the displayed estimate.
\end{proof}
