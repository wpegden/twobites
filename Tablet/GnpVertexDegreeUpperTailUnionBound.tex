\begin{helper}[GnpVertexDegreeUpperTailUnionBound]
Let \(m\in\mathbb N\), \(0\le p\le 1\), \(\epsilon,t\in\mathbb R\) with
\(0\le t\), and \(r\in\operatorname{Fin}(m)\).  Put
\[
I_r=\{\,e=\{a,b\}: a,b\in\operatorname{Fin}(m),\ a<b,\ a=r
\text{ or } b=r\,\}.
\]
Under the \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}\) product graph weight, the degree at
\(r\) obeys the two Markov--MGF bounds
\[
\sum_G
\begin{cases}
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G),
&(1+\epsilon)p\,m\le \noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r),\\
0,&\text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\le
\exp\!\bigl(-t(1+\epsilon)p\,m\bigr)
\bigl(1-p+p\exp(t)\bigr)^{|I_r|}
\]
and
\[
\sum_G
\begin{cases}
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G),
&\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)\le (1-\epsilon)p\,m,\\
0,&\text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\le
\exp\!\bigl(t(1-\epsilon)p\,m\bigr)
\bigl(1-p+p\exp(-t)\bigr)^{|I_r|}.
\]
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
For each graph \(G\), the map that sends a neighbor \(w\) of \(r\) to the
ordered edge \((r,w)\) when \(r<w\), and to \((w,r)\) when \(w<r\), is a
bijection from the neighbors counted by \(\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}\) to the
present incident edge coordinates in \(I_r\).  Hence
\[
\exp\!\bigl(u\,\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)\bigr)
=
\prod_{e\in I_r}
\begin{cases}
\exp(u),&e\text{ is present in }G,\\
1,&e\text{ is absent in }G.
\end{cases}
\]
Summing this identity against \(\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G)\) and applying
\(\noderef{GnpVertexIncidentEdgeGeneratingFunction}\) with \(z=\exp(u)\)
gives
\[
\sum_G \exp\!\bigl(u\,\noderef{GraphDegreeCount}(G,r)\bigr)
\noderef{GnpGraphWeight}(p,G)
=
\bigl(1-p+p\exp(u)\bigr)^{|I_r|}.
\]
Apply \(\noderef{GnpVertexDegreeMarkovTailBounds}\) with
\(A=(1+\epsilon)p\,m\) and use the displayed identity with \(u=t\) for the
upper tail.  Apply the same Markov bound with \(A=(1-\epsilon)p\,m\) and use
the displayed identity with \(u=-t\) for the lower tail.
\end{proof}
