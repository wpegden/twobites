\begin{helper}
For all natural numbers \(n,K\), if \(n>0\), then
\[
\binom nK\le \exp(K\log n)
\]
after viewing both sides as real numbers.
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
The standard binomial estimate gives
\[
\binom nK\le n^K.
\]
Casting this inequality to the real numbers preserves the inequality.  Since
\(n>0\), the real power identity gives
\[
n^K=\exp(K\log n).
\]
Combining these two facts proves the claim.
\end{proof}
