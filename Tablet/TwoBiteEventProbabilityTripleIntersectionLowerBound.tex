\begin{helper}
Let \(E,F,G\) be events in one two-bite sample space.  If all sample weights
are nonnegative and the total mass of the whole sample space is at most \(1\),
then
\[
\Pr(E)+\Pr(F)+\Pr(G)-2 \le \Pr(E\cap F\cap G),
\]
where \(\Pr\) denotes \(\noderef{TwoBiteEventProbability}\).
\end{helper}
\begin{proof}
Write the probability of an event as the finite sum of its indicator times the
sample weight.  For each sample \(\omega\), the pointwise indicator inequality
\[
1_{E\cap F\cap G}(\omega)
\ge 1_E(\omega)+1_F(\omega)+1_G(\omega)-2
\]
holds after multiplication by the nonnegative weight, except that the constant
\(-2\) contributes \(-2\) times the sample weight.  Summing over all samples
therefore gives
\[
\Pr(E\cap F\cap G)\ge
\Pr(E)+\Pr(F)+\Pr(G)-2\Pr(\mathrm{True}).
\]
The assumed total-mass bound \(\Pr(\mathrm{True})\le1\) implies
\(-2\Pr(\mathrm{True})\ge-2\), and hence the displayed lower bound.
\end{proof}
