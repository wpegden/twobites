\begin{lemma}
Let \(H,k,t_1,p,m,\varepsilon_1\) be real numbers.  If
\[
H\le \frac{2k}{t_1},\qquad
1\le 2pm,
\]
and
\[
\left(\frac{2k}{t_1}\right)\noderef{RealChooseTwo}(2pm)
\le \varepsilon_1 k^2,
\]
then
\[
H\,\noderef{RealChooseTwo}(2pm)\le \varepsilon_1 k^2.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Put \(D=2pm\) and \(Q=\noderef{RealChooseTwo}(D)\).  From \(1\le D\) we have
\(0\le D\) and \(0\le D-1\).  By the definition of
\(\noderef{RealChooseTwo}\),
\[
Q=\frac{D(D-1)}2,
\]
so \(Q\ge0\).

Multiplying the hypothesis \(H\le 2k/t_1\) by the nonnegative real number
\(Q\) gives
\[
H Q\le \left(\frac{2k}{t_1}\right)Q.
\]
The third hypothesis gives
\[
\left(\frac{2k}{t_1}\right)Q\le \varepsilon_1 k^2.
\]
Transitivity of the order on real numbers therefore gives
\[
H\,\noderef{RealChooseTwo}(2pm)=H Q\le \varepsilon_1 k^2,
\]
as required.
\end{proof}
